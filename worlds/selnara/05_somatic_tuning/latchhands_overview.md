# Latchhands / Somatic Tuning ("Magic")

## What It Is

**Latchhands** (colloquial) or **Somatic Tuning** (technical name) is the skill of **shaping how one's body interacts with Signal Pale vapor/flow**.

This is a **real physical skill with trainable neuro-muscular technique** (not a supernatural "gift"). It happens because:

* Signal Pale passes through tissue and nerves
* Repeated exposure builds **sensory discrimination and muscle mapping**
* Specific postures, grips, breath rhythms, and fingertip pressure patterns can **catch, mirror, deflect, or amplify** Signal Pale behavior within the body long enough to "nudge" how Signal Pale behaves in close proximity

## Not a Spell System

This is **not casting spells**. It's closer to "piano technique" or "gunfighter draw speed":

* Requires drilling
* Relies on posture discipline
* Needs ongoing exposure to maintain sensitivity
* Fails without clean Signal Pale environment

## How It Manifests

### Somatic Trigger

* A specific hand/finger configuration
* A breath hold or release
* A tension pattern in the jaw or shoulders
* A postural lock (weight shift, spine angle)

### Pale Behavior Response

Signal Pale near the practitioner **momentarily responds** to the operator's nervous/vascular feedback:

* **Sense-loop**: Sharpen hearing, taste metal in air, feel pressure gradients
* **Nerve-echo**: Mimic opponent's involuntary flinch timing (steal "tells")
* **Drift-push**: Cause brief vertigo or false motion sensation in target
* **Lock-pull**: Stabilize shaking hands or override fatigue response

## Key Constraints (LOCKED)

### 1) Range: Touch-to-Five-Paces

**No battlefield wizardry.** This is hallway/duel/close-quarters work.

### 2) Duration: Seconds to a Minute Max

These are **bursts**, not sustained states. Technique quality determines how long the effect can be held before muscle collapse or nerve overload.

### 3) Reliance on Signal Pale Concentration

The environment must have adequate Signal Pale presence:

* Telegraph stations, repeaters, and signal corridors = rich environments
* Refineries and dock warehouses = moderate
* Open desert or deep underground = weak/unreliable

### 4) Physiological Cost

Each use:
* Burns energy rapidly (hunger, shaking)
* Requires recovery time
* Cumulative fatigue over multiple uses
* Risk of nerve damage from overextension

### 5) Skill Training Required

* Years of exposure to Signal Pale
* Formal technique drilling
* Master-apprentice transmission
* Continuous practice to maintain sensitivity

## Evidence and Recognition

Latchhand practitioners develop characteristic signs:

* **Fingertip Calluses**: Specific pressure patterns
* **Posture Discipline**: Distinctive movement economy
* **Breath Control**: Visible thoracic discipline
* **Gaze Steadiness**: Reduced startle reflex

Experienced opponents can recognize technique initiation by watching for:
* Finger placement shifts
* Sudden stillness
* Breath hold
* Weight shift into ready stance

## Long-Term Degradation (Territory Loss)

Prolonged Somatic Tuning causes progressive **territory loss** across three domains:

### Somatic Territory (Body)

* Galvanic scarring patterns on palms/forearms
* Chronic tremors, seizures, tinnitus
* Smell/taste degradation
* Nerve pathway damage becomes permanent

### Cognitive Territory (Mind)

* **Preference drift**: Repeated Signal work makes practitioners calmer, more compliant, less urgency-capable
* **Memory shearing**: Missing minutes, wrong sequencing, false certainty about events
* **Contested will**: Difficulty determining if desires are authentic or installed

### Dream Territory (Sleep)

* **Dream-bleed**: Prolonged exposure causes practitioners to share dream content with others who worked the same circuits
* **Circuit memory**: Dreams contain protocols never learned, grief for people never met, knowledge that shouldn't exist
* **Residue sleep**: Not telepathy — contamination. The circuit remembers; the Latchhand carries it.

**The Horror**: A veteran Latchhand cannot reliably distinguish their own memories, preferences, or dreams from those absorbed through their work. Their "professional calm" may be damage, not discipline.

## Social Consequences of Territory Loss

* Enemies call them "softened" or "looped"
* Allies fear dependence on their compliance
* Testimony becomes unreliable (time-sense drift)
* Institutions exploit cognitive vulnerability
* Long-term practitioners become ideal employees — and cannot be certain they chose to be
