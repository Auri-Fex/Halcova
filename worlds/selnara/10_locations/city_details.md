## ZEVAKALAK (The Sea-City)

*Population: ~180,000 | Primary Hub: Trade, Administration*

### Districts

**The Wharves** (Working Class):
- Sprawling dock complex, city's economic heart
- Warehouse districts, loading yards, fisheries
- Worker tenements stacked above businesses
- Smells of salt, fish, coal smoke, tar
- *Landmarks*: Great Crane (steam-powered), Fish Market Hall

**Syndic Hill** (Elite):
- Elevated quarter, deliberate separation
- Great House compounds behind walls
- Manicured streets, private security
- Clean air, clean water, clean conscience
- *Landmarks*: Beghelis-Kalak Compound, Conclave Hall

**The Tangle** (Mixed/Poor):
- Organic growth between planned districts
- Narrow streets, improvised housing
- Mixed commerce, marginal businesses
- Where people go to disappear
- *Landmarks*: None official; "The Broken Anchor" tavern known locally

**Enclave Central** (Neutral):
- Standard Enclave headquarters
- Planned grid, uniform architecture
- Administrative buildings, archives
- Artificially ordered amid urban chaos
- *Landmarks*: The Measure Hall, Central Archive, Inspection Courts

**Telegraph Row** (Commercial):
- Communication infrastructure hub
- Broker offices, message services
- Constant activity, day and night
- Humming wires overhead
- *Landmarks*: Central Signal Station, Zevakalak Exchange

**The Drowning** (Slum):
- Lowest elevation, floods regularly
- Poorest residents, worst conditions
- Criminal operations tolerated
- City services minimal
- *Landmarks*: None acknowledged; locals know "The Pit" fighting venue

### Key Locations

**The Great Crane**:
- Steam-powered loading crane, symbol of city
- Can lift entire cargo holds
- Operated by hereditary crane families
- Tourist attraction and critical infrastructure

**Beghelis-Kalak Compound**:
- Largest Syndic residence
- Behind thirty-foot walls
- Internal gardens, authentication vaults
- Where succession drama unfolds

**The Measure Hall**:
- Standard Enclave governing center
- Seven Directors meet here
- Public courts on ground floor
- Archives in basement levels

**Lado's Place** (The Tangle):
- Tavern near Navsad Pier
- Information broker headquarters
- Average drinks, premium secrets
- Neutral ground by reputation

### Local Flavor

**Street food**: Wrapped fish, vapor broth, salt-cured vendors
**Local drink**: Zevaraki (harsh grain spirit), better taverns have metavin
**Common sounds**: Ship bells, crane steam, market calls, seabirds
**Common sights**: Oilcloth coats, Syndic badges, foreign sailors
**Local saying**: "The sea gives, the sea takes, the Syndics count."

## SHAREM (The Terrace-City)

*Population: ~95,000 | Primary Hub: Extraction, Processing*

### Districts

**Upper Terraces** (Elite):
- Highest levels, best air quality
- Broker management, wealthy merchants
- Elevator access points controlled here
- Views of the pit below
- *Landmarks*: Terrace Garden (only green space), Director's Platform

**The Rings** (Working/Middle):
- Concentric residential levels
- Worker housing, small commerce
- Descending quality with depth
- Most of the population lives here
- *Landmarks*: Ring Market (spiral marketplace), Third Ring Chapel

**The Workface** (Industrial):
- Extraction operation areas
- Refining facilities, storage
- Highest contamination levels
- Workers live adjacent to work
- *Landmarks*: Primary Extraction Head, Manifold Station Seven

**Deep Sharem** (Slum/Criminal):
- Lowest inhabited levels
- Abandoned by city services
- Criminal operations, desperate poor
- Contamination levels dangerous
- *Landmarks*: None official; "The Seep" is known gathering place

**The Seal** (Restricted):
- Sharem Terrace Seal site (312 PE)
- Quarantine zone, fifteen years running
- Military presence, research teams
- Nobody goes in who doesn't have to
- *Landmarks*: The Seal Wall, Observation Posts

### Vertical Navigation

**Elevator System**:
- Six major lift lines
- Controlled by Broker consortium
- Expensive for workers (1 puli per ride)
- Elite have private access

**The Stairs**:
- Free but exhausting
- Main Stair: 3,000+ steps surface to deep
- Local stairs between adjacent levels
- "Stair runners" carry messages for tips

**Rope Ways**:
- Cargo transport between levels
- Some passenger use (dangerous, cheap)
- Operated by independent crews

### Key Locations

**The Seal Wall**:
- Fifteen-year quarantine barrier
- What happened in 312 PE still unclear
- Research teams study from observation
- Workers avoid discussing it

**Manifold Station Seven**:
- Largest processing facility
- Where Raw Pale becomes Charged Pale
- Heavily secured, contamination protocols
- Director Maia Khergemi's showcase

**Ring Market**:
- Spiral marketplace descending three levels
- Everything available, quality varies with depth
- Upper ring: legitimate goods
- Lower ring: "no questions asked"

**The Seep** (Deep Sharem):
- Gathering place in lowest levels
- Built around natural Pale Medium seepage
- Criminal operations, black market
- Pit Boss Vakhtang Sharemi's territory

### Local Flavor

**Street food**: Terrace stew, root skewers, ashcake
**Local drink**: Sharem Black (pit-brewed beer), "Varnish" for the desperate
**Common sounds**: Extraction machinery, elevator bells, echoing voices
**Common sights**: Dust masks, contamination suits, vertical perspectives
**Local saying**: "Depth is destiny. Climb or die."

## KARESHTUL (The Gate-City)

*Population: ~65,000 | Primary Hub: Transit, River Control*

### Districts

**The Locks** (Industrial):
- Lock-fort complexes controlling river
- Massive infrastructure, constant operation
- Worker housing integrated with machinery
- Sound of water, always
- *Landmarks*: Great Lock (largest), Lock-Master's Tower

**Toll Town** (Commercial):
- Built around transit fees
- Inns, warehouses, services for travelers
- Transient population, permanent businesses
- Money changes hands constantly
- *Landmarks*: Tollhouse Square, Trader's Rest (largest inn)

**River Quarter** (Mixed):
- Permanent residents along waterfront
- Fishers, boat operators, dock workers
- Strong community identity
- Suspicious of outsiders
- *Landmarks*: River Chapel, Fisher's Market

**The Narrows** (Working/Poor):
- Cramped housing between lock complexes
- Workers without lock-operator status
- Services for lock workers
- Confined, claustrophobic streets
- *Landmarks*: None official; "The Spillway" tavern is local center

**Syndic Wharf** (Elite):
- Beghelis-Karesh compound
- Transit documentation center
- Elevated above flood levels
- Controls who moves through
- *Landmarks*: Beghelis-Karesh House, Documentation Hall

### Lock System

**The Great Lock**:
- Largest river lock in Selnara
- Can lift/lower vessels 40 feet
- Three-hour operation cycle
- Choke point for river trade

**Lock-Forts**:
- Fortified control points
- Military and commercial combined
- Lock-Masters hold significant power
- Hereditary positions in some cases

**Transit Fees**:
- Charged per vessel, per cargo type
- Beghelis-Karesh documentation required
- Smuggling attempts constant
- Lock workers bribed frequently

### Key Locations

**Great Lock**:
- Engineering marvel, political symbol
- Tour destination for visitors
- Security perimeter enforced
- Lock-Master Tornike Kareshis commands

**Documentation Hall**:
- Where transit papers are authenticated
- Bottleneck for legitimate trade
- Bribes, delays, frustrations
- Matriarch Rusudan personally oversees

**Tollhouse Square**:
- Commercial center of Toll Town
- Money changers, services, information
- Where deals are made
- Navsad Pier maintains presence

**The Spillway** (The Narrows):
- Worker tavern, union organizing
- Lock workers meet here
- Owners sympathetic to labor
- Syndics watch but can't act openly

### Local Flavor

**Street food**: Locksman's plate, river fish, caravan bread
**Local drink**: River ale (mild, refreshing), stronger options in Spillway
**Common sounds**: Lock machinery, water rushing, transit bells
**Common sights**: Blue-and-grey uniforms, transit papers, river vessels
**Local saying**: "Everything flows through Kareshtul. Everything."

## METASAFEK (The Storm-City)

*Population: ~40,000 | Primary Hub: Charging, Research*

### Districts

**The Frames** (Industrial):
- Skyframe installation complexes
- Highest elevation in city
- Dangerous during storms, essential during storms
- Worker housing below frame level
- *Landmarks*: Primary Array, Storm Tower

**Research Quarter** (Elite/Technical):
- Technical Directorate headquarters
- Laboratories, testing facilities
- Restricted access, security enforced
- Where manifold secrets are kept
- *Landmarks*: Directorate Hall, Experimental Station

**Storm Town** (Working):
- Worker housing, services
- Built to withstand weather
- Communal facilities, shared protection
- Strong crew solidarity
- *Landmarks*: Storm Shelter (communal refuge), Crew Hall

**The Cables** (Mixed):
- Built along power distribution lines
- Mixed commercial, residential
- Constant low-level discharge exposure
- Distinctive architecture (grounded, insulated)
- *Landmarks*: Cable Market, The Grounded (tavern)

**Downhill** (Lower Class):
- Lowest elevation, safest from direct storm
- But also furthest from work, lowest status
- Service workers, disabled, retired
- Subsistence living
- *Landmarks*: None official; "The Shelter" is community center

### Weather Integration

**Storm Schedule**:
- Charging operations weather-dependent
- Storm season: maximum activity, maximum danger
- Calm season: maintenance, reduced crews

**Storm Warnings**:
- Signal tower system
- Mandatory shelter protocols
- Those caught out during storms die
- Schedule disruptions have cascading effects

**Grounding Infrastructure**:
- Entire city wired for discharge protection
- Failure means fire, death
- Maintenance crews critical
- "Grounders" are respected profession

### Key Locations

**Primary Array**:
- Largest skyframe installation
- Twelve charging spires
- Director Vakhtang Khergemi's pride
- Best safety record, still has casualties

**Storm Tower**:
- Weather observation, storm prediction
- Critical for scheduling
- Staffed continuously
- Operators have peculiar reputation

**Directorate Hall**:
- Technical Directorate headquarters
- Research laboratories below
- Restricted access, secrets inside
- Chief Maia Khergemi rarely leaves

**The Grounded** (The Cables):
- Tavern for technical workers
- Named for its exceptional grounding
- Safe even during storms
- Information exchange hub

### Local Flavor

**Street food**: Storm porridge, wrapped meat, dense bread
**Local drink**: Steadyhands (worker stimulant), storm-brewed beer
**Common sounds**: Wind, discharge crackle, warning bells, cable hum
**Common sights**: Insulated clothing, grounding straps, weather-watching
**Local saying**: "Respect the storm. It respects nothing."

## KIDSHET (The Edge-City)

*Population: ~25,000 (variable) | Primary Hub: Frontier Trade*

### Districts

**The Market** (Commercial):
- Sprawling trade bazaar
- Temporary structures, permanent chaos
- Everything available, nothing guaranteed
- Where frontier meets civilization
- *Landmarks*: Great Tent (market center), Caravan Yard

**Broker's Row** (Elite):
- Khergem and Syndic outposts
- Fortified compounds
- Minimal presence, maximum control attempts
- Frontier grudgingly tolerates them
- *Landmarks*: Broker Compound, Syndic Station

**The Camps** (Transient):
- Caravan staging areas
- Temporary housing, seasonal populations
- Varies wildly by season and trade
- No permanent structures
- *Landmarks*: None permanent; locations shift

**Old Town** (Permanent):
- Original settlement core
- Permanent residents, established families
- Suspicious of all outsiders equally
- Frontier independence culture
- *Landmarks*: Founder's Well, Town Hall (rarely used)

**The Edge** (Marginal):
- Beyond organized settlement
- Squatters, desperate, hiding
- No services, no protection
- Where people go when nowhere else
- *Landmarks*: None; deliberately unmapped

### Frontier Dynamics

**Seasonal Population**:
- Winter: ~15,000 (core residents)
- Trade season: ~35,000+ (caravans, traders)
- Constant flux of faces

**Authority Vacuum**:
- Formal authority weak
- Reputation and force matter
- Disputes settled personally or through respected locals
- Navsad Pier presence minimal

**Survival Culture**:
- Self-reliance valued
- Mutual aid among trusted
- Strangers prove themselves
- "Frontier justice" is real

### Key Locations

**Great Tent**:
- Massive market structure
- Rebuilt annually
- Where major deals happen
- Neutral ground by custom

**Founder's Well**:
- Original settlement water source
- Sacred to permanent residents
- Community gathering point
- Outsiders drink there at invitation only

**Broker Compound**:
- Khergem frontier outpost
- Expansion Front operations
- Director Sandro's personal project
- Locals resent but need it

**The Waystation**:
- Caravan services, information
- Run by Medea Kidani (permanent resident)
- Knows everyone, trusts few
- Closest thing to neutral authority

### Local Flavor

**Street food**: Caravan bread, dried meat, whatever's available
**Local drink**: Whatever traders bring, local ferments
**Common sounds**: Animals, bargaining, wind, distance
**Common sights**: Desert wraps, weapons openly carried, mixed origins
**Local saying**: "The edge takes care of its own. Are you its own?"
