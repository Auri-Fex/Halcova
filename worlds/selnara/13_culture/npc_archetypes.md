# NPC Archetypes

## Status: LOCKED

Character templates for populating Selnara with memorable, consistent NPCs. Each archetype includes role, motivations, typical conflicts, and naming conventions.

---

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

### Georgian-Derived Name Patterns

**Given names**: Often derive from roots or have traditional feel
- Male endings: -i, -o, -an, -el
- Female endings: -a, -i, -ela, -ani
- Gender-neutral: -is, -en

**Family names**: Often indicate:
- Trade origin: Beghelis (seal-worker), Khergemi (spine-worker)
- Location origin: Zevari (from sea), Sharemi (from Sharem)
- Ancestor deed: Kalakeni (city-founder)

**Example names by class**:
| Class | Male | Female | Surname |
|-------|------|--------|---------|
| Worker | Davit, Lado, Tornike | Nana, Maia, Tamar | Metani, Dinari, Kavari |
| Merchant | Giorgi, Levan, Sandro | Salome, Ketevan, Elene | Beghelis, Navsadis, Kalakeni |
| Elite | Vakhtang, Sulkhan, Nikoloz | Tinatin, Rusudan, Medea | Khergemi, Zevari, Kareshis |

## BEGHEL SYNDIC ARCHETYPES

### The House Heir

**Role**: Young Syndic being groomed for family leadership

**Characteristics**:
- Education in authentication, house politics
- Pressure to prove worth before inheriting
- Often sent on "proving missions" to frontier offices
- May be idealistic or cynically ambitious

**Motivations**:
- Earn family respect
- Prove superior to rival heirs
- Possibly reform corrupt practices (idealist variant)
- Secure advantageous marriage alliance

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Family expectations vs. personal desires
- Rival heirs within same house
- Discovering uncomfortable truths about family business
- Romance across house lines

**Example**: **Levan Beghelis** — Third son of minor Zevakalak house, sent to Kidshet frontier office to "build character." Secretly sympathetic to workers' conditions.

---

### The Authentication Master

**Role**: Senior expert in beghel verification and fraud detection

**Characteristics**:
- Decades of experience reading seals
- Knows forgery techniques better than forgers
- Repository of house secrets and scandals
- Often physically distinctive (eye strain, ink-stained hands)

**Motivations**:
- Maintain house reputation
- Uncover fraud before it damages clients
- Protect authentication standards
- Personal pride in craft

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Asked to authenticate questionable documents
- Discovers fraud within own house
- Blackmail based on knowledge of secrets
- Rivalry with younger, less scrupulous authenticators

**Example**: **Ketevan Zevari** — Sixty years with House Beghelis-Kalak, can spot a forged seal by touch alone. Knows where too many bodies are buried.

---

### The House Enforcer

**Role**: Handles "difficult" matters for Syndic family

**Characteristics**:
- Not officially employed by house (deniability)
- Mix of intimidation, investigation, and violence
- Often former military or criminal background
- Operates in grey legal areas

**Motivations**:
- Loyalty to employer (or payment)
- Personal code of conduct (varies)
- Protecting house interests
- Avoiding own past catching up

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Orders that cross personal lines
- Targets who don't deserve it
- Other enforcers from rival houses
- Law enforcement interest

**Example**: **Tornike Sharemi** — Former Sharem pit enforcer, now handles debt collection and "persuasion" for House Beghelis-Minor. Has rules about children.

## KHERGEM BROKER ARCHETYPES

### The Skyframe Captain

**Role**: Commands charging crew on ridge installations

**Characteristics**:
- Weather-reading expertise
- Crew leadership under dangerous conditions
- Physically marked by profession (static scars, weathered skin)
- Superstitious about storms and omens

**Motivations**:
- Crew survival
- Meeting charging quotas
- Maintaining equipment (limited budgets)
- Personal reputation among peers

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Pressure to work in unsafe conditions
- Crew members taking risks
- Equipment failures during critical windows
- Broker management demanding impossible output

**Example**: **Vakhtang Metani** — Twenty years on Metasafek skyframes, lost two fingers to discharge. His crew trusts him more than any manifest schedule.

---

### The Manifold Operator

**Role**: Skilled technician managing Pale Medium transformation

**Characteristics**:
- Technical expertise in dangerous processes
- Constant low-level Signal exposure
- Careful, methodical personality (survivors are careful)
- Often has mild contamination symptoms

**Motivations**:
- Technical pride in work
- Supporting family on hazard pay
- Understanding the process better
- Eventually getting off the manifolds

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Safety vs. production pressure
- Contamination anxiety
- Knowledge of unsafe practices
- Training replacements (emotional burden)

**Example**: **Maia Khergemi** — Manifold technician for twelve years, hands shake slightly now. Knows her station better than the engineers who designed it.

---

### The Ridge Assessor

**Role**: Evaluates charging sites, equipment, and output potential

**Characteristics**:
- Combines technical and business knowledge
- Travels frequently to remote installations
- Often the bearer of bad news (site closures, budget cuts)
- Sees both worker conditions and profit margins

**Motivations**:
- Accurate assessments (professional pride)
- Career advancement within Khergem
- Possibly advocating for better conditions
- Personal enrichment through side deals

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Pressure to falsify assessments
- Seeing conditions that should be reported
- Bribery offers from desperate site managers
- Caught between workers and management

**Example**: **Sandro Kareshis** — Young assessor with engineering background. Keeps getting sent to sites about to be shut down. Starting to see patterns.

## STANDARD ENCLAVE ARCHETYPES

### The Weights and Measures Inspector

**Role**: Ensures trade fairness through standardized measurements

**Characteristics**:
- Precise, detail-oriented personality
- Often underestimated (just a clerk with scales)
- Actually has significant power to shut down trade
- Repository of market information

**Motivations**:
- Fair markets (idealist)
- Bribes and side income (corrupt variant)
- Professional advancement
- Leverage over merchants

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Pressure to overlook violations
- Discovering systematic fraud
- Threats from those being inspected
- Jurisdictional disputes with Syndics

**Example**: **Giorgi Navsadis** — Inspector in Zevakalak's fish markets for fifteen years. Everyone knows him. Everyone's careful when he's watching.

---

### The Protocol Arbiter

**Role**: Determines which regulations apply to edge cases

**Characteristics**:
- Encyclopedic knowledge of Enclave rules
- Comfortable with bureaucratic authority
- Often sought for informal advice
- May be rigid or pragmatically flexible

**Motivations**:
- Consistent application of rules
- Protecting Enclave authority
- Personal interpretation of "fairness"
- Political positioning within Enclave

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Cases that don't fit existing rules
- Pressure from powerful parties
- Subordinates who disagree
- Rival arbiters with different interpretations

**Example**: **Rusudan Kalakeni** — Senior arbiter in Kareshtul. Her interpretations become precedent. She knows this and chooses carefully.

---

### The Enclave Guard

**Role**: Security and enforcement within Standard zones

**Characteristics**:
- Military or martial background
- Clear jurisdiction (Enclave grounds only)
- Often frustrated by limits of authority
- Professional relationship with other forces

**Motivations**:
- Maintaining order
- Steady employment and benefits
- Pride in Enclave service
- Possibly ambition for rank

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Crimes that cross jurisdictional lines
- Pressure from Syndics to look away
- Pursuit that ends at Enclave borders
- Internal corruption

**Example**: **Davit Dinari** — Guard-sergeant in Zevakalak Enclave. Good at his job, bitter about criminals who escape by crossing property lines.

## NAVSAD PIER ARCHETYPES

### The Neutral Arbitrator

**Role**: Judges disputes between parties

**Characteristics**:
- Reputation for fairness (or sale to highest bidder)
- Deep knowledge of precedent and custom
- Often former merchant, Syndic, or worker (varied backgrounds)
- Distinctive robes or markers of office

**Motivations**:
- Justice according to their understanding
- Maintaining Navsad reputation
- Personal legacy of decisions
- Income from arbitration fees

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Cases where both sides have valid claims
- Pressure from powerful parties
- Evidence that's been manipulated
- Personal bias they must overcome

**Example**: **Sulkhan Navsadis** — Arbitrator for thirty years, known for ruling against Syndics when evidence warrants. Has survived three assassination attempts.

---

### The Pier Advocate

**Role**: Represents clients in Navsad proceedings

**Characteristics**:
- Rhetorical skill and precedent knowledge
- Operates on fees or contingency
- Range from prestigious to desperate
- Often specialized (labor disputes, trade claims, inheritance)

**Motivations**:
- Client success (reputation-driven)
- Fee income
- Genuine belief in client's case
- Building precedent for future cases

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Clients who are clearly wrong
- Cases against powerful opponents
- Ethical lines in advocacy
- Payment disputes with clients

**Example**: **Elene Metani** — Worker-advocate in Sharem. Takes cases others won't. Lives modestly because her clients can't pay much.

---

### The Information Broker

**Role**: Gathers and sells information relevant to disputes

**Characteristics**:
- Network of sources across classes
- Officially neutral (sells to all sides)
- Knows which secrets have value
- Often operates from Pier-adjacent establishments

**Motivations**:
- Profit from information trade
- Leverage for personal protection
- Understanding the full picture
- Possibly ideological goals (exposing corruption)

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Information that's dangerous to possess
- Clients who want sources revealed
- Competing brokers
- Becoming target due to knowledge

**Example**: **Lado Sharemi** — Runs a tavern near Zevakalak's Navsad Pier. The drinks are average. The information is premium.

## LATCHHAND ARCHETYPES

### The Veteran Circuiteer

**Role**: Experienced Signal manipulator, survivor of the craft

**Characteristics**:
- Galvanic scarring, possibly tremors
- Deep technique knowledge, hard-won
- Cautious about teaching (knows the costs)
- Respected and feared in equal measure

**Motivations**:
- Passing on knowledge (selective)
- Finding sustainable technique use
- Protecting younger latchhands
- Understanding what they've become

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Students who won't listen to warnings
- Requests for dangerous techniques
- Own declining abilities
- Younger latchhands who judge their caution

**Example**: **Nikoloz Khergemi** — Was one of the best. Now teaches, mostly. His hands don't work like they used to, but his knowledge is priceless.

---

### The Wild Talent

**Role**: Natural ability with minimal training

**Characteristics**:
- Instinctive technique, poor theory
- Often young, often reckless
- High potential, high risk
- May not understand what they're doing

**Motivations**:
- Testing limits of ability
- Recognition and status
- Understanding their gift
- Escaping whatever they came from

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Technique effects they can't control
- Established latchhands threatened by their talent
- Broker recruitment pressure
- Burning out before learning caution

**Example**: **Tamar Kavari** — Seventeen, from Kidshet frontier. Can feel discharge patterns before they happen. Doesn't know why, doesn't know the danger.

---

### The Broker-Bound Specialist

**Role**: Latchhand under contract to Khergem operations

**Characteristics**:
- Regular work, regular exposure
- Specific technique specialty
- Contractual obligations limit freedom
- Often the most stable employment available

**Motivations**:
- Steady income and protection
- Paying off training debt
- Eventually buying out contract
- Professional reputation within specialty

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Dangerous assignments
- Contract terms that seem unfair
- Offers from competitors
- Technique demands exceeding safe limits

**Example**: **Medea Zevari** — Charging specialist for Metasafek operations. Three years left on her contract. Good at her job, counting the days.

## WORKER ARCHETYPES

### The Extraction Crew Boss

**Role**: Manages work team in Pale Medium extraction

**Characteristics**:
- Authority over crew safety and output
- Caught between management and workers
- Knows the work intimately
- Often shows early contamination signs

**Motivations**:
- Crew survival (primary)
- Meeting quotas (required)
- Fair treatment for workers
- Own survival and exit strategy

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Unsafe conditions ordered by management
- Workers who take risks
- Contamination decisions (who works where)
- Accusations from either side

**Example**: **Lado Metani** — Crew boss in Sharem Terrace operations. Thirty workers under him. Argues with management daily about safety. Drinks heavily.

---

### The Telegraph Operator

**Role**: Sends and receives messages through Signal infrastructure

**Characteristics**:
- Technical skills with equipment
- Low-level constant exposure
- Hears everything, says nothing (professionally)
- Often develops hearing problems

**Motivations**:
- Steady skilled employment
- Information advantage (valuable)
- Understanding the network
- Protecting operator solidarity

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Pressure to reveal message contents
- Corrupted transmissions (who's responsible?)
- Equipment failures during critical moments
- Knowing things they shouldn't

**Example**: **Nana Dinari** — Chief operator in Kareshtul. Has worked the keys for twenty years. Her memory for messages is legendary and terrifying.

---

### The Dock Worker

**Role**: Loads, unloads, and moves cargo in port cities

**Characteristics**:
- Physical strength and endurance
- Detailed knowledge of shipping
- Solidarity with fellow workers
- Casual relationship with smuggling

**Motivations**:
- Daily wage
- Family support
- Worker organizing (some)
- Side income opportunities

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Dangerous cargo (contaminated goods)
- Strike actions and consequences
- Smuggling pressures (participate or inform)
- Competition for limited work

**Example**: **Tornike Zevari** — Senior docker in Zevakalak. Knows which crates to avoid. Knows which bosses can be trusted. Both lists are short.

## CRIMINAL ARCHETYPES

### The Smuggler Captain

**Role**: Moves illegal or untaxed goods

**Characteristics**:
- Ship or caravan command
- Network of contacts and bribes
- Risk calculation expertise
- Often charming, always calculating

**Motivations**:
- Profit margins on illegal trade
- Freedom from legitimate authority
- Protecting crew and operation
- Eventually going legitimate (or not)

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Syndic enforcement
- Competing smugglers
- Crew loyalty questions
- Cargo that's worse than expected

**Example**: **Giorgi Kidani** — Runs goods along the frontier routes. Doesn't ask what's in the crates. Asks who's paying and how much.

---

### The Forgery Artist

**Role**: Creates false beghels, documents, and identities

**Characteristics**:
- Exceptional technical skill
- Understanding of authentication systems
- Paranoid operational security
- Often artistic temperament

**Motivations**:
- Craft pride (even in crime)
- High fees for skilled work
- Evading Syndic detection
- Possibly ideological (helping people escape)

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Clients who get caught and might talk
- Syndic authentication improvements
- Competitors who undercut quality
- Jobs that serve terrible purposes

**Example**: **Salome Navsadis** — Creates new identities for those who need them. Charges rich clients extra to subsidize refugees. Never meets clients in person.

---

### The Pit Boss

**Role**: Controls illegal operations in underworld areas

**Characteristics**:
- Territorial authority through force
- Network of enforcers and informants
- Often legitimate business as front
- Complex relationships with authorities

**Motivations**:
- Territorial control and expansion
- Profit from various rackets
- Respect and fear from community
- Eventually going legitimate (some)

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Rival organizations
- Enforcement pressure
- Subordinates' ambitions
- Moral lines (if any remain)

**Example**: **Vakhtang Sharemi** — Controls three terrace levels in deep Sharem. Runs protection, gambling, and Dregs distribution. Grew up in those tunnels.

## SPECIALIST ARCHETYPES

### The Contamination Physician

**Role**: Treats Signal and Pale exposure effects

**Characteristics**:
- Medical training plus specialized knowledge
- Sees the worst outcomes of industry
- Often in high demand, poor areas
- May experiment with treatments

**Motivations**:
- Helping patients (idealist)
- Research into contamination (curious)
- Income from desperate clients
- Understanding the fundamental problem

**Typical Conflicts**:
- More patients than can be treated
- Treatments that don't work
- Pressure to clear workers for unsafe return
- Knowledge of systemic problems

**Example**: **Tinatin Beghelis** — Doctor in Sharem's mid-terraces. Treats miners who can't afford better. Keeps detailed notes on contamination patterns.

---

### The Memory Clerk

**Role**: Records testimony and maintains written memory

**Characteristics**:
- Precise documentation skills
- Understanding of memory shearing
- Often employed by Navsad Piers or Syndics
- Trusted with sensitive information

**Motivations**:
- Accuracy of record
- Employment security
- Understanding what's really happening
- Possibly leveraging knowledge

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Pressure to alter records
- Testimony that contradicts documentation
- Memory shearing in witnesses
- Knowing too much

**Example**: **Levan Kareshis** — Memory clerk for Zevakalak's Navsad Pier. His records have decided dozens of cases. He remembers every one.

---

### The Salvage Specialist

**Role**: Recovers value from dangerous or contaminated materials

**Characteristics**:
- High-risk, high-reward work
- Expertise in contamination assessment
- Often independent or small crews
- Superstitious about sites and materials

**Motivations**:
- Big scores from recovered materials
- Independence from Broker contracts
- Thrill of dangerous work
- Eventually getting out with enough

**Typical Conflicts**:
- Dangerous sites and materials
- Claim disputes with others
- Fencing recovered goods
- Contamination from work

**Example**: **Davit Siremis** — Works the dead zones around Siremsha. Knows which sites are worth the risk. Has the scars to prove his estimates aren't always right.
