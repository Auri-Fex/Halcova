# Social Structures

> **Quick Summary**: Norbela's society is rigidly stratified into seven castes, from omnipotent barons to disposable slaves, where access to magical oil determines life quality, lifespan, and basic dignity.

**In this article:**
- The Seven Castes
- Social Mobility
- Cultural Norms
- Power Structures
- Daily Realities

---

## The Seven Castes

### Overview

**Rigid Stratification**: Birth mostly determines position
**Magic = Status**: Access to shyak correlates perfectly with social tier
**Legal Inequality**: Different laws apply to different castes
**Population Distribution**:
- Shyakor (Elite): ~1%
- Meshkorin (Professionals): ~8%
- Telkorin (Workers): ~25%
- Korbal (Laborers): ~35%
- Drenkor (Indentured): ~15%
- Finyakor (Outcasts): ~10%
- Vordesh (Slaves): ~6%

---

## 1. Shyakor (Arcane Elite)

**Population**: ~82,000 (~1%)
**Definition**: Barons, high officials, major guildmasters, wealthy merchants

### Privileges

**Complete**:
- Unlimited shyak access (personal reserves)
- Private armies
- Immunity from common law
- Own extraction territories
- Access to forbidden knowledge

### Daily Life

**Morning**:
- Wake in silk sheets
- Servants dress them
- Exotic breakfast (continent-wide imports)

**Daytime**:
- Council meetings
- Guild negotiations
- Plot against rivals
- Political dinners

**Evening**:
- Decadent parties
- Drug use (refined shyak derivatives)
- Theater, orchestras
- Sexual exploitation

**Night**:
- Sleep in anti-entropy chambers (slow aging)

### Dark Reality

**Casual Cruelty**:
- Beat servants for entertainment
- Sexual exploitation of lower castes
- Blood sport entertainment (prisoners vs creatures)
- Casual murder (covered up by bought officials)

**Addiction**:
- Pure shyak access = dependency common
- Magical addiction epidemic among elite
- No consequences for behavior

**Political Games**:
- Incestuous alliances (intermarry)
- Buy/sell positions
- Eliminate rivals creatively

**Life Expectancy**: 80-100 years (with magic)

---

## 2. Meshkorin (Skilled Professionals)

**Population**: ~656,000 (~8%)
**Definition**: Artificers, scholars, physicians, officers, established merchants

### Privileges

**Limited**:
- Regular shyak rations (work, not luxury)
- Legal protections (can sue in courts)
- Own property
- Guild access
- Travel freely (with papers)

### Daily Life

**Morning**:
- Wake in modest homes
- Small arcane heating (one room)
- Bread, preserved meat, tea

**Daytime**:
- 10-12 hour workday
- Guildhalls, academies, workshops, clinics
- Guild meetings
- Politics within profession

**Evening**:
- Family time (if can afford children)
- Study
- Guild social events

**Night**:
- Sleep in warded beds (basic entropy protection)

### Struggles

**Constant Pressure**:
- Must produce or fall to lower castes
- Guild dues 30-40% of income
- Backstabbing for promotions
- Choose between heating and work fuel

**Burnout**:
- Suicide rates high
- Many addicted to low-grade shyak (performance enhancers)
- Sell children into apprenticeships
- Constant bribery to maintain position

**Life Expectancy**: 50-60 years

---

## 3. Telkorin (Common Workers)

**Population**: ~2,050,000 (~25%)
**Definition**: Skilled laborers, small merchants, low soldiers, scribes, nurses

### Privileges

**Minimal**:
- Minimal shyak rations (survival amounts)
- Basic legal protections (rarely enforced)
- Can rent housing
- Primary education access

### Daily Life

**Morning**:
- Wake in cramped apartments (4-6 per room)
- Gruel, stale bread, water

**Daytime**:
- 12-14 hour workday
- Shops, markets, barracks, factories
- No breaks
- Street food if affordable

**Evening**:
- Exhausted collapse
- Maintain tools
- Family obligations

**Night**:
- Straw mattresses
- Cold
- Constant noise

### Reality

**Never Enough**:
- Perpetual debt
- One illness from poverty
- Children work age 8-10
- Live week-to-week

**Dark Elements**:
- Petty crime to survive
- Domestic violence common
- Alcoholism rampant
- Sell possessions, sometimes family
- Sex work supplements income

**Life Expectancy**: 45-50 years

---

## 4. Korbal (Unskilled Laborers)

**Population**: ~2,870,000 (~35%)
**Definition**: Factory workers, miners, dockworkers, servants, rank-and-file soldiers

### Status

**No Privileges**:
- No shyak rations (exposed through work)
- No legal protections
- Cannot own property
- No education
- Cannot travel without permission

### Daily Life

**Morning**:
- Wake in barracks/tenements (10-15 per room)
- Gruel if lucky; often skip

**Daytime**:
- 14-16 hour brutal labor
- Mines, refineries, docks, farms, factories
- Whatever employer provides (spoiled, minimal)

**Evening**:
- Too exhausted to do anything
- Collapse

**Night**:
- Sleep on floors, in shifts
- Cold
- No privacy

### Survival Only

**Constant**:
- Hunger (malnutrition universal)
- Injuries (no medical care unless employer pays)
- Death from overwork, accidents, exposure

**Expendable**:
- Seen as disposable
- Debt traps (company stores)
- Families broken up

**Hard R Reality**:
- Industrial accidents horrific
- Sexual assault by overseers common
- Survival sex work
- Children born into servitude

**Life Expectancy**: 35-40 years

---

## 5. Drenkor (Indentured Servants)

**Population**: ~1,230,000 (~15%)
**Status**: Legally property for contract duration (5-20 years, often extended)

### Mechanisms of Indenture

**Debt**:
- Borrow money
- Can't repay
- Sign contract
- Trap extends indefinitely

**Crime**:
- Caught stealing bread
- Choice: indenture or execution

**Refugee**:
- Flee dying regions
- Border guards "rescue" (indenture)
- Sign or die

**Orphan**:
- No guardian
- State assigns to employer
- Work until age 25

### Daily Life

**Whenever Ordered**:
- Pre-dawn wake
- Scraps from owner's table
- Whatever work demanded
- Beatings for disobedience
- Sleep wherever allowed

**Generational**:
- Children of indentured = indentured
- Breeding programs (create more servants)
- Suicide = only escape

**Life Expectancy**: 25-35 years

---

## 6. Finyakor (Outcasts)

**Population**: ~820,000 (~10%)
**Definition**: Exiles, mutants, criminals, heretics, diseased

### No Legal Existence

**Can Be Killed Without Consequence**:
- No rights
- No protections
- No recourse

### Survival

**Scavenging**:
- Ruins, sewers, forests, caves
- Rats, roots, garbage, corpses
- Hide from everyone
- Constant violence

**Many Mutated**:
- Entropy corruption (extra limbs, glowing eyes, madness)
- Cannibalism confirmed
- Form desperate gangs
- Disease rampant

**Life Expectancy**: 15-25 years

---

## 7. Vordesh (Slaves)

**Population**: ~492,000 (~6%)
**Status**: Legal property

### Sources

- Captured in war
- Born to slaves
- Sold by desperate families
- Criminals (life sentences)

### Uses

- Mining (2-5 year lifespan)
- Agriculture (5-10 years)
- Domestic labor
- Sex work
- Gladiatorial combat (1-3 years)
- Medical experiments

### Daily Life

**No Agency**:
- Wake to bell or whip
- Fed once or twice (minimal)
- 16-18 hours work under guard
- Beatings routine
- Sleep in pens, cages, chains

**Families Destroyed**:
- Separated immediately
- Children sold age 5
- Rebellions crushed (mass executions)

**Life Expectancy**: 2-10 years (depending on use)

## Social Mobility

### Upward (Rare)

**Possible Paths**:
- Exceptional talent + luck (artificer invents something revolutionary)
- Marriage into higher caste (rare, usually women)
- Military achievement (promotion through ranks)
- Criminal success (Nethvor-Shadows recruitment)

**Probability**: <1% per generation

### Downward (Common)

**Easy Falls**:
- Illness (medical bankruptcy)
- Injury (can't work)
- Debt (spirals into indenture)
- Crime (even minor)
- Political disfavor
- Dead zone expansion (refugees become outcasts)

**Probability**: 15-20% fall one tier in lifetime

### Locked Tiers

**Cannot Rise From**:
- Indentured (until contract ends, if ever)
- Slave (only via escape, rare)
- Outcast (permanent stigma)

**Cannot Fall From** (easily):
- Shyakor (too powerful, protected)

## Cultural Norms

### Greetings

**Between Equals**: Hand clasp, use names
**To Superiors**: Bow, avert eyes, use title
**To Inferiors**: Nod or ignore

**Taboos**:
- Never touch higher caste without permission
- Never look baron in eyes (execution)
- Never refuse direct order

### Naming

**Upper Castes**: Full conlang + lineage (Shalen-Mor tal Kalen-Sheloth)
**Middle Castes**: Name + occupation (Grenash-Vel Kelkorin)
**Lower Castes**: Single name (Dren, Kesh, Vorn)
**Outcasts**: Numbers, insults, chosen names (Scar, Twisted)
**Slaves**: Owner assigns; birth names erased

## Power Structures

### Official

**Baron Council**: Nine barons rule
**Guild System**: Controls professions
**Military**: Enforces order
**Courts**: Baron's justice (bought)

### Unofficial

**Nethvor-Shadows**: True power (information, blackmail)
**Criminal Guilds**: Control vice, smuggling
**Religious Leaders**: Influence masses
**Rebel Groups**: Fight (ineffectively)
