# Tavanor (The Scholar's Domain)

> **Quick Summary**: Tavanor is the political and intellectual center of Norbela, home to the High Archon and largest city, scarred by a massive central dead zone from the Second Cascade but still the wealthiest and most stable region.

**Size**: ~2.5 million km²
**Population**: 3 million
**Capital**: Kalen-Sheloth (900,000)
**Barons**: Shalen-Mor (High Archon), Telvor-Shin (Crystal Caverns)
**Dead Zones**: 20%
**Climate**: Mildest region (-5°C to 10°C)

---

## Geography

**Terrain**:
- Temperate frost forests (northern and eastern regions)
- Crystal mountain ranges (Telvor-Shin's territory)
- Ancient ruins scattered throughout
- Coastal areas in east (ports)
- Central dead zone (500,000 km², Second Cascade site)

**Major Features**:
- **Stanyak Ral** (Crystal Mantle Ranges): Mountains with massive crystal formations
- **Central Tavanor Dead Zone**: Former capital site, Texas-sized wasteland
- **Eastern Coastline**: Ports and fishing villages
- **Kalen River System**: Provides water and transport

**Habitability**: 80% habitable (excluding dead zone)

---

## Cities and Settlements

**Kalen-Sheloth** (900,000):
- Capital of Tavanor and continent
- Shalen-Mor's palace and government
- Arcane Academy (1,000 students)
- Sky Beacon (navigational landmark)
- Council Hall (where nine barons meet)
- Massive slums (400,000 in tenements)

**Telian-Kresh** (150,000):
- Eastern port city
- Arcane shipyards
- Fishing industry
- Smuggling hub (Nethvor-controlled)

**Gen-Sorath** (80,000):
- Mountain valley city
- Telumesh Archive (1M+ texts)
- **Under siege by Inquisition** (1 month, starving)
- Crystal mines nearby

**Smaller Settlements** (50+ towns, 1,000-10,000 each):
- Farming communities
- Mining outposts
- Academic satellites
- Border fortresses

---

## Economy

**Primary Industries**:
- Arcane academia and research (largest in world)
- Government and administration
- Banking and finance
- Crystal mining (Telvor-Shin monopoly)
- Mixed creature extraction (20% of continental shyak)

**Trade**:
- Hub for all continental commerce
- Rail network center
- Banking houses
- Import: Raw materials, food
- Export: Finished goods, knowledge, crystals

**Employment**:
- Government: 15%
- Academia: 10%
- Trade/Commerce: 25%
- Manufacturing: 20%
- Agriculture: 15%
- Other: 15%

---

## Politics

**Dual Baron Rule**:

**Shalen-Mor** (High Archon):
- Rules Kalen-Sheloth and western territories
- Chair of Baron Council
- Moderate reformist (by baron standards)
- Supports limited creature protections
- Opposes Koresh-Val's expansion

**Telvor-Shin** (Crystal Baron):
- Rules eastern mountains and caverns
- Controls crystal trade monopoly
- Mystic and visionary
- Sees futures in crystal meditations
- Politically neutral (claims)

**Internal Conflicts**:
- Conservative vs reformist factions
- Extraction quotas enforcement
- Inquisition overreach
- Refugee policy (Konor crisis)
- Dead zone expansion planning

---

## Culture

**Academic Dominance**:
- Literacy: 60% (highest on continent)
- Universities and libraries throughout
- Respect for knowledge and scholarship
- Meritocracy (in theory)

**Class Structure**:
- **Elite** (5%): Nobles, high mages, wealthy merchants
- **Middle** (35%): Scholars, artisans, merchants, officials
- **Lower** (60%): Workers, farmers, servants

**Art and Philosophy**:
- Blue aesthetic (prevalent cold light)
- Arcane-punk architecture
- Political theater and debate
- Philosophical schools

**Religion**:
- Diverse faiths tolerated
- Inquisition presence (increasing)
- Some creature worship (taboo)

---

## Military

**Tavanor Guard** (30,000 regulars):
- Defending Kalen-Sheloth
- Border patrols
- Anti-bandit operations
- Well-equipped (arcane weapons)

**City Militias** (50,000 total):
- Local defense
- Variable quality
- Conscription in emergencies

**Mercenaries**:
- Hired for special operations
- Often from Vashor (Threnok-Gash)

**Threats**:
- Morshan-Breakers (bombings in capital)
- Bandit raids (trade routes)
- Inquisition overreach (internal)
- Dead zone expansion

---

## Dangers

**Second Cascade Dead Zone**:
- 500,000 km² instant death zone
- Created 1872, killed 100,000+
- Former capital buried inside
- Artifacts worth millions (unreachable)
- Expansion slow but steady

**Political Intrigue**:
- Assassinations common
- Factional violence
- Inquisitor witch hunts
- Breakers terrorism

**Economic**:
- Wealth inequality extreme
- Slum conditions deadly
- Disease outbreaks
- Food shortages (periodic)

---

## Story Integration

**Plot Hooks**:
1. **Archive Siege**: Gen-Sorath falls; priceless texts lost or saved?
2. **Crystal Prophecy**: Telvor-Shin's vision predicts disaster; believe or dismiss?
3. **Undercity**: Discover ancient ruins beneath Kalen-Sheloth; treasures and dangers
4. **Dead Zone Expansion**: Central dead zone moving; evacuate districts
5. **Political Coup**: Faction attempts to overthrow Shalen-Mor; civil war looms

**For Stories**:
- Central to most political plots
- Academic mysteries and research
- Class conflict and inequality
- Dead zone horror nearby
- Refugee crisis (Konor influx)
