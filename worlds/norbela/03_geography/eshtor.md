# Eshtor (The Living Forest)

> **Quick Summary**: Eshtor is the largest and most biodiverse region, home to mega-forests and the last healthy ecosystem, currently being invaded by Koresh-Val while conservationists and indigenous Vornalen-Keepers fight a losing guerrilla war.

**Size**: ~3 million km² (largest region)
**Population**: 2 million (highly dispersed)
**Capital**: Contested (Vorn-Merath on Sulenor border)
**Cultural Center**: The First Grove (hidden Vornalen-Keeper sanctuary)
**Barons**: Velasha-Nen (forests), Dreshak-Mor (swamps)
**Dead Zones**: 15% (lowest, due to conservation)
**Climate**: Humid and cold (-5°C to 15°C)

---

## Geography

**Vorn Mega-Forest** (1 million km²):
- Trees 100-200 meters tall (Blaykor live in canopies)
- Crystal growths throughout
- Dense undergrowth
- Creature-rich ecosystem
- Multiple layers (canopy, mid-story, floor)

**Fungal Jungles**:
- Massive fungal towers 10-30m tall
- Perpetual mist and fog
- High humidity despite cold
- Bioluminescent species
- Toxic spores

**Swamp Systems**:
- Extensive wetlands
- Seasonal flooding
- Disease vectors
- Hidden channels
- Dreshak-Mor's territory

**River Networks**:
- 70% of continent's potable water
- Strategic resource
- Transport routes
- Fishing grounds

---

## Settlements

**The First Grove** (~5,000-10,000):
- Vornalen-Keeper sanctuary
- Location hidden
- Built into mega-trees
- Creature sanctuaries
- Outsiders killed on sight

**Dresh-Kalmor** (200,000):
- Dreshak-Mor's capital
- Built on stilts over swamp
- Canals and waterways
- Smuggling hub
- Vice and corruption center
- Disease-ridden

**Forest Villages** (100+ settlements, 500-5,000 each):
- Sustainable extraction
- Hidden from Koresh-Val
- Guerrilla bases
- Refugees from invasion

---

## Economy

**Creature Extraction** (20% of continental shyak):
- Blaykor (Luminbark Colossus) - sacred to Keepers
- Forest fungal creatures
- Swamp species
- **Conservation-focused** (Velasha-Nen's policy)
- Sustainable yields

**Resources**:
- Lumber (mega-trees valuable)
- Alchemical ingredients (fungi, plants)
- Food production (60% with Tavanor)
- Fresh water (70% of continent's supply)

**Strategic Value**:
- Last healthy ecosystem
- Water source (potential weapon)
- Food basket
- Creature populations

---

## Politics

**Three-Way Conflict**:

**Velasha-Nen** (Conservationist):
- Protects forests
- Limits extraction
- Allies with Keepers (uneasy)
- Defending against Koresh-Val

**Dreshak-Mor** (Criminal):
- Controls swamps
- Smuggling and vice
- Neutral in forest war
- Profits from both sides

**Vornalen-Keepers** (Indigenous):
- Kill outsiders on sight
- Protect sacred groves
- Alliance with Velasha-Nen (temporary)
- Attack both Koresh-Val and civilians

**Koresh-Val's Invasion**:
- Wants water and creatures
- Scorched earth tactics
- Civilian massacres
- Environmental destruction

---

## Culture

**Forest Dwellers**:
- Respect for nature
- Sustainable practices
- Oral traditions
- Community-focused

**Keepers**:
- Shamanic society
- Creature worship
- Xenophobic
- Warrior-druids
- Speak own language (archaic conlang)

**Swamp Folk**:
- Survivors and outcasts
- No morals (survival)
- Criminal culture
- Disease tolerance

---

## Military

**Velasha-Nen's Forces** (20,000):
- Forest rangers
- Guerrilla fighters
- Allied with Keepers (10,000 warriors)
- Defensive war

**Koresh-Val's Invasion** (30,000):
- Professional soldiers
- Arcane weapons
- Superior equipment
- Brutal tactics

**Vornalen-Keepers** (Unknown strength):
- Ambush masters
- Creature allies
- Know every path
- No mercy

**Current Status**: Stalemate, but Velasha-Nen slowly losing

---

## Dangers

**Vornalen-Keepers**:
- Kill outsiders instantly
- Arrow in throat
- Hung from trees as warning
- 200+ travelers killed annually

**Invasion Violence**:
- Villages burned
- Civilians massacred
- Children bayoneted
- Scorched earth

**Environmental**:
- Disease (40% infection rate, 60% fatality)
- Toxic fungi
- Predatory creatures
- Getting lost (common, fatal)

**Swamp Hazards**:
- Quicksand
- Toxic water
- Parasites
- Criminals

## Story Integration

**Plot Hooks**:
1. **First Grove**: Find sanctuary; negotiate with Keepers or die trying
2. **Water War**: Koresh-Val dams rivers; ecological and political disaster
3. **Keeper Alliance**: Unite factions against invasion or watch forest fall
4. **Sacred Tree**: Ancient Blaykor dying; save it or lose Keeper support
5. **Swamp Passage**: Smuggle through Dresh-Kalmor; vice and violence
6. **Village Massacre**: Witness or prevent; moral choices
7. **Last Stand**: Final battle for forest; victory or extinction

**Themes**: Conservation vs exploitation, indigenous rights, environmental destruction, guerrilla warfare
