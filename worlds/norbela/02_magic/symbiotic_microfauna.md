# Symbiotic Microfauna

## The Microbe-Host Relationship

**True Source of Magic**:
- Host creatures **cannot** produce shyak directly
- Symbiotic microbes do all the work
- Host provides habitat, nutrients, transport
- Microbes provide magical oil
- Mutually dependent relationship

**Location**:
- Specialized organs and glands
- Digestive tract
- Blubber and fat tissues
- Sometimes integrated throughout body

**Benefits to Host**:
- Access to finyak energy (warmth, power)
- Competitive advantage in ecosystem
- Enhanced abilities
- Longer lifespan in some cases

**Benefits to Microbes**:
- Safe habitat
- Nutrient supply
- Transportation to finyak sources
- Protection from environment

---

## How Microbes Metabolize Finyak

**The Process** (simplified):
1. Host consumes nutrients (food)
2. Host absorbs atmospheric or geological finyak
3. Microbes metabolize both together
4. Complex biochemical reactions occur
5. Magical lipids and proteins produced
6. Secreted as shyak into host glands

**Requirements**:
- Organic nutrients from host diet
- Trace finyak exposure (air or water)
- Stable host environment
- Proper microbe population balance
- Time (continuous process)

**Chemical Magic**:
- Exact mechanism still mysterious
- Involves arcane biochemistry
- Different microbe strains = different oil properties
- No artificial synthesis achieved

---

## Microbe Ecology

**Population Dynamics**:
- Billions per host
- Multiple species often present
- Complex interactions and competition
- Balance critical for oil quality

**Reproduction**:
- Rapid (like Earth bacteria)
- Within host continuously
- Some transfer between hosts (unclear how)
- Genetic drift and evolution

**Species Diversity**:
- Different microbes for different creatures
- Strains specialized to host species
- Some generalists, most specialists
- New species still being discovered

**Outside Host**:
- Cannot survive long independently
- Need living tissue environment
- All cultivation attempts failed
- Die within hours outside host

---

## Threats to Microbes

**Disease**:
- Microbe-specific pathogens exist
- Can wipe out colonies
- Host survives but loses magic
- Epidemics possible

**Stress**:
- Over-extraction weakens microbes
- Poor host health affects production
- Environmental toxins harmful
- Starvation kills microbes first

**Pollution**:
- Magical runoff toxic to microbes
- Chemical contamination deadly
- Entropy exposure fatal
- Accumulates over time

**Genetic Bottlenecks**:
- Over-harvesting reduces diversity
- Inbreeding in captive populations
- Loss of genetic variation
- Increased disease susceptibility

---

## Conservation Implications

**Microbes Are the Key**:
- Without microbes, no shyak ever
- Creature populations matter less than microbe health
- Conservation must protect both

**Breeding Programs**:
- Must maintain microbe diversity
- Cross-contamination risks
- Genetic monitoring essential
- More complex than just creature breeding

**Restoration Attempts**:
- Cannot introduce microbes to dead zones
- No finyak = no food = microbes die
- Catch-22 for recovery
- Some try anyway (all failed)

**The Hidden Crisis**:
- Microbe health declining globally
- Less visible than creature decline
- May be point of no return
- Few studying it seriously
