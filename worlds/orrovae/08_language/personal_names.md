# ORROVAE PERSONAL NAMING CONVENTIONS

## Overview

Orrovae's naming traditions reflect 10,000 years of underground adaptation, class stratification, and cultural divergence. Three distinct naming traditions exist, each carrying social and political weight.

---

## THE THREE NAMING TRADITIONS

### Tradition A: Germanic-Industrial (Guildtongue)

**Real-world basis**: German, Dutch, Scandinavian languages
**Cultural association**: Guilds, established institutions, Midwork power structures
**Demographics**: 50% of population; dominant in Midworks and industrial districts
**Social signal**: Authority, institutional legitimacy, technical competence

**Phonology**:
- Consonants: Hard stops (k, t, p), fricatives (f, v, s, z, sh), liquids (r, l)
- Consonant clusters: br-, gr-, kr-, tr-, -st, -sk, -cht
- Vowels: a, e, i, o, u (short and long)
- Stress: Usually first syllable

**Name Patterns**:
- **Male-coded**: Often end in -en, -or, -us, -mar, -ik, -ast
- **Female-coded**: Often end in -a, -el, -is, -et, -in
- **Unisex**: Single syllable or -en ending

---

### Tradition B: Anglo-Saxon Revival (Oldblood)

**Real-world basis**: Old English, pre-Norman Anglo-Saxon
**Cultural association**: Ancient families, Crownwalk lineages, conservative doctrine adherents
**Demographics**: 20% of population; concentrated in upper tiers and old settlements
**Social signal**: Pre-Scouring heritage, nobility, cultural preservation

**Phonology**:
- Consonants: Full Old English range including eth (√∞), thorn (√æ), ash (√¶)
- Common patterns: wulf-, ead-, god-, alf-, cyne-
- Vowels: a, e, i, o, u, √¶, y
- Compound structure: Two meaningful elements

**Name Patterns**:
- **Male-coded**: -ric, -win, -mund, -heard, -berht, -wulf
- **Female-coded**: -wyn, -hild, -gyth, -fl√¶d, -run, -gifu
- **Compound meanings**: Always significant (e.g., Wulfric = "wolf-power")

---

### Tradition C: Finno-Ugric Hybrid (Hollowspeech / Sovereign-tongue)

**Real-world basis**: Finnish, Estonian, Basque languages
**Cultural association**: Unseeded communities, frontier settlements, Plain Condition adherents
**Demographics**: 30% of population; majority unseeded, also surface runners and fringe communities
**Social signal**: Outsider status, sovereignty movement, rejection of mainstream power

**Phonology**:
- **Finnish-influenced**: Vowel harmony, double consonants (kk, tt, ll)
  - Vowels: a, e, i, o, u, y, √§, √∂
  - Consonants: k, t, p, s, h, l, r, n, m, j, v
- **Basque-influenced**: Unusual clusters (tx, tz, ts), x as "sh"
  - Consonants: k, t, x, z, r, l, n, m, b, d, g
- **Hybrid features**: Mix both for maximum variety

**Name Patterns**:
- **Mostly ungendered**: Same name can be used by anyone
- **Patronymic**: -n or -in suffix (Kalevin = "son/child of Kalev")
- **Diminutive**: -i ending (Kaisi from Kaisa)

## STORYTELLING GUIDANCE

### When to Use Each Tradition

**Germanic-Industrial** (50% of names):
- **Use for**: Authority figures, guild members, established families, Midworks majority
- **Tone**: Institutional, formal, predictable, respectable
- **Social signal**: "I have a place in the system"
- **Example scenarios**: Guild halls, official documents, Writ-Doctor practices, Midworks markets

**Anglo-Saxon Revival** (20% of names):
- **Use for**: Old families, Crownwalk elite, conservative factions, pre-Scouring descendants
- **Tone**: Ancient, noble, traditionalist, culturally precious
- **Social signal**: "My bloodline predates the Scouring"
- **Example scenarios**: Crownwalk ceremonies, noble houses, historical documents, traditionalist communities

**Finno-Ugric Hybrid** (30% of names):
- **Use for**: Unseeded populations, frontier settlements, sovereignty movements, outsiders
- **Tone**: Independent, foreign, resistant, egalitarian
- **Social signal**: "I reject your power structure"
- **Example scenarios**: Unseeded enclaves, frontier holds, resistance cells, sovereignty movements

### Class Mixing

**Upper Class Never Uses**: Finno-Ugric tradition (seen as lower/outsider)
**Middle Class Rarely Uses**: Anglo-Saxon full compounds (seen as pretentious)
**Lower Class Never Uses**: Anglo-Saxon tradition (inaccessible/mocked)
**Unseeded Rarely Uses**: Germanic tradition (rejection of mainstream)

### Name Changes

**Upward Mobility**:
- Finno-Ugric ‚Üí Germanic (common among ambitious unseeded who seek seeding)
- Germanic simple ‚Üí Germanic formal (guild advancement)
- Never Germanic/Finno-Ugric ‚Üí Anglo-Saxon (bloodline cannot be bought)

**Downward Mobility**:
- Anglo-Saxon ‚Üí Germanic (fallen families, economic collapse)
- Germanic ‚Üí descriptor only (loss of guild standing, exile)
- Any ‚Üí single name (Hollowing, exile, criminal status)

**Hollowing Inversion**:
- Pre-Hollowing: "Grath Torven" with S‰idesolu "Eisenherz"
- Post-Hollowing: "Eisenherz" (primary identity) with "of Grath" or "who was Grath"

### Regional Variations

**Established Holds** (large cities):
- 60% Germanic, 25% Anglo-Saxon, 15% Finno-Ugric

**Frontier Settlements** (new/small):
- 35% Germanic, 5% Anglo-Saxon, 60% Finno-Ugric

**Mining Outposts**:

**Unseeded Enclaves**:
