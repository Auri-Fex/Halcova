---

## BOOK SUMMARY

Year four of the Maiming Wars. The "Unmaimed" doctrine that justified so much suffering begins to collapse under its own contradictions—but its death throes are violent. Kris Halvard, Torven's successor, dies in what she believes is a peace negotiation but is actually a trap designed to remove the resistance's most effective commander. Kaisa Kalevin's communication with the surface creatures is discovered; she's not executed, but she's politically destroyed—her career ended, her reputation ruined, her knowledge dismissed as collaboration. The constructs, denied personhood, begin to take action rather than ask questions. And underground, the war turns inward as the doctrine's defenders fight to preserve their power even as their ideology crumbles.

---

## THEMATIC FOCUS

**Question**: When the lie that held everything together breaks, what does the wreckage reveal?
**Answer Suggested**: The truth was always there; the lie just gave people permission to ignore it
**Dark Night**: The doctrine's death doesn't end suffering—it creates a vacuum that might be filled by something worse
