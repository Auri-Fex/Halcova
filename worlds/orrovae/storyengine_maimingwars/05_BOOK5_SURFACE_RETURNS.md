**Macro Function**: RESOLUTION — "The Surface Wins" — Reunion, not invasion

---

## BOOK SUMMARY

Five years since the first maiming weapons were deployed. The Maiming Wars end not because one side wins, but because a third party returns to claim their inheritance. The surface creatures breach the gates—not to attack, but to enter the home their ancestors were driven from ten thousand years ago. When the underground collapses the gates in desperation, the creatures reveal what Kaisa has known all along: they're not invaders. They're the descendants of those abandoned on the surface when the underground sealed itself against a dying sun. They want to come home. The final battle isn't human vs. surface creature. It's humans vs. humans over whether to accept their kin back. By the time it's over, Kaisa Kalevin—the surface-runner who learned to speak with the "enemy"—is the only POV still alive. She stands at the opened gate, translating for both sides, carrying the memory of everyone who died to get here.

---

## THEMATIC FOCUS

**Question**: When everything you built was built on forgetting, what do you owe those you forgot?
**Answer Suggested**: You owe them the truth. You owe them choice. You might even owe them home.
**Resolution**: The surface "wins"—but winning means reunion, not conquest
