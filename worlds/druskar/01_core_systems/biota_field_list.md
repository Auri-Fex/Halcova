## Detailed Entries

### 1. CHRONOPHAGE (Time Fly)
**Category:** Chronomass source organism  
**Habitat:** Kronvar Belt charter farms (cultivated); Jitknal Labyrinth (wild traps)  
**Use:** Primary source of harvestable chronomass—metabolizes time-adjacent gradients and excretes resin that can be stabilized  
**Hazard:** Residue outbreaks when handling goes wrong; wild populations are a public hazard  
**Tell:** Banded wick-darkening in assay lanterns  
**Note:** Street people call them "time flies" because it's easier than "chronophage" and because the farms look like fly works. They are not mascots—they are a supply chain and a public hazard.

### 2. SETSTARTER (Starter)
**Category:** Processing organism
**Habitat:** Aselvek assay/setter benches
**Use:** Biological component that stabilizes Raw chronomass into Set grade
**Hazard:** Delayed lot failure if starter culture is compromised
**Tell:** Late vent-bloom scars on affected lots

### 3. BLEKKULT (Bleach)
**Category:** Industrial organism
**Habitat:** Brinefield industrial flats; Aselvek decon lanes
**Use:** Decontamination and antiseptic applications
**Hazard:** Lung-burn vapor in concentrated exposure
**Tell:** Pale brass film on metal; throat rash in workers

### 4. FILTMAT (Mat)
**Category:** Filter organism
**Habitat:** Respirators and vent systems
**Use:** Living biological filters
**Hazard:** Spore shedding causes assay false positives
**Tell:** Lantern flags contamination but grades don't match actual state

### 5. RESINVET (Resin)
**Category:** Seal organism
**Habitat:** Seal Houses
**Use:** Source of tamper-evident seal resin
**Hazard:** Pest damage; counterfeit resin culture
**Tell:** Fracture geometry mismatch in fake seals

### 6. BINDERMIT (Mites)
**Category:** Pest
**Habitat:** Bonded yards
**Use:** None (pest)
**Hazard:** Can be used to manufacture false "tamper" evidence
**Tell:** Uniform nibble-line pattern on seal edges (too regular)

### 7. TOTHEEL (Teeth)
**Category:** Pest
**Habitat:** Gate housings and lock mechanisms
**Use:** None (pest)
**Hazard:** Jams gate teeth; bites maintenance divers
**Tell:** Distinctive grease smear pattern + brass scoring marks

### 8. CHAINSNAIL (Chalk)
**Category:** Biofouling organism
**Habitat:** Chains and governors
**Use:** None (biofoul)
**Hazard:** Increases drag; causes governor slip
**Tell:** Chalk-white spiral pattern on chain links

### 9. SILTWASP (Pin)
**Category:** Pest
**Habitat:** Braces and spill courts
**Use:** None (pest)
**Hazard:** Weakens braces through nesting; tremor-inducing stings
**Tell:** Pinhole nest lines in structural members + sting clusters on workers

### 10. BLOOMGNAT (Gnat)
**Category:** Bloom-associated organism
**Habitat:** Brinav March during bloom season
**Use:** Rare additive (niche applications)
**Hazard:** Causes assay contamination and false readings
**Tell:** Sticky sheen on lantern glass; wet-iron smell

### 11. ROTKELP (Rot)
**Category:** Industrial organism
**Habitat:** Brackwater flats
**Use:** Membranes and packing material
**Hazard:** Releases confusion-inducing off-gas when mishandled
**Tell:** Black-green film under fingernails; distinctive cloth odor

### 12. SHELLSTOCK (Shell)
**Category:** Food organism
**Habitat:** Trough farms
**Use:** Protein source
**Hazard:** Rapid spoilage causes gut-sickness outbreaks
**Tell:** Sickness clusters correlated with specific trough stamps

### 13. GELSTOCK (Gel)
**Category:** Industrial organism
**Habitat:** Vat houses
**Use:** Protein and glue production
**Hazard:** Contamination spreads rapidly through batches
**Tell:** Clear-brown stringing residue on processing tools

### 14. TOWBEAST (Tow)
**Category:** Draft animal
**Habitat:** Knalvam market; Luskil lock village
**Use:** Barge hauling on towpaths
**Hazard:** Panic behavior during chronomass envelope events
**Tell:** Distinctive hoof-splay prints when panicked

### 15. WICKMOLD (Wick)
**Category:** Domestic organism
**Habitat:** Wick heaters in homes and businesses
**Use:** Provides heat stability in fuel wicks
**Hazard:** Sweet-smelling soot fumes (long-term exposure risk)
**Tell:** Distinctive soot signature; sweet smell in hair and clothing

## Legal Consequence

**Quarantine is not cosmetic.** It exists because endemic biota can:
- Ruin chronomass grade
- Compromise seal integrity
- Destabilize lockworks
- Create plausible deniability for seizures
