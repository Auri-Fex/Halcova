## Selected Languages

| Role               | Language     | Reasoning                                                                                                                                                                      |

| **Root**     | Hungarian    | Rich vocabulary for abstract and visceral concepts. Agglutinative structure provides flexible roots. Unique among European languages—feels ancient and unfamiliar.            |
| **Affix**    | Arabic       | Distinct phonetic character. Tri-consonantal roots allow for varied affix extraction. Carries inherent weight and formality. Religious/mystical connotations fit dark fantasy. |
| **Mutation** | Scots Gaelic | Soft mutations create flowing transformations. Celtic character adds otherworldly quality. Balances Hungarian's harshness with musicality.                                     |

---

## Language Character

**Overall Feel**: Heavy, formal, weighty. Words feel like they carry consequence. Religious undertones without specific religious reference. Sounds like a language used for binding oaths, recording debts, naming the dead.

**Phonetic Tendencies**:

- Hard consonants from Hungarian (k, t, sz, gy)
- Guttural sounds from Arabic (kh, q, gh)
- Soft mutations from Gaelic (bh→v, mh→v, ch→soft ch)
- Long vowels with accent marks for stress

**Syllable Patterns**:

- Prefer 2-3 syllables for common words
- Longer forms preserved for formal/ritual use
- Compound words compress heavily

## Affix Variations (Documented)

To avoid repetitive endings, multiple affixes derived from Arabic for common concepts:

### Soul/Spirit Affixes

- *-ruh* (spirit) → -rù, -ru
- *-nafs* (self/soul) → -naf, -nav
- *-qalb* (heart/core) → -calb, -calv

### Death/End Affixes

- *-mawt* (death) → -maht, -mabht
- *-fana* (annihilation) → -fan, -fana
- *-nihaya* (end) → -niha, -nia

### Binding/Connection Affixes

- *-qayd* (chain) → -caidh, -cai
- *-rabt* (tie) → -rabht, -rav
- *-'ahd* (covenant) → -ahd, -àhd

### Body/Physical Affixes

- *-lahm* (flesh) → -laimh, -lav
- *-jism* (body) → -gism, -gis
- *-jasad* (corpse) → -jasad, -jas

### Fate/Path Affixes

- *-qadr* (destiny) → -cadr, -cad
- *-nasib* (fortune) → -nasiv, -nav
- *-tariq* (path) → -taric, -tar

## Usage Notes

1. **Formal vs. Common**: Longer unmolted forms preserved for ritual, legal, and religious contexts. Molted forms for everyday speech.
2. **Compounds**: When combining concepts, use the shorter affix variants and compress aggressively.
3. **Names**: Personal names follow same construction but may use different affix sets to indicate family, profession, or origin.
4. **Loanwords**: Concepts from other magic systems may retain more of their original structure to mark them as "foreign" to native speakers.
