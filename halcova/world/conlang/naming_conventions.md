# Naming Conventions of Hálcova

## The Five-Part Name Structure

Every person in Hálcova may possess up to five names, acquired at different life stages:

| Position | Name Type | Acquired | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|----------|---------|
| 1st | **Given Name** | Birth | Identity; parental hopes |
| 2nd | **Virtue Name** | Age 7 | Observed character |
| 3rd | **Fate Name** | Binding (15) | Pattern designation |
| 4th | **Craft Name** | Apprenticeship/Practice | Professional identity |
| 5th | **Line Name** | Inherited | Family/ancestry |

**Usage varies by context:**
- Full formal: All five (legal documents, major ceremonies)
- Standard formal: Given + Fate + Line (most official contexts)
- Common address: Given + Line (daily interaction among privileged)
- Working address: Given + Craft (among laborers and practitioners)
- Intimate: Given only (family, close friends)

---

## Name Construction Process

All names follow the established conlang rules:

**Step 1**: Hungarian root (meaning)
**Step 2**: Arabic affix (category/quality)
**Step 3**: Gaelic mutation (softening)
**Step 4**: Molting (compression for common use)

### Mutation Rules Applied

| Original | Mutated | Sound |
|----------|---------|-------|
| k | c | k sound retained |
| p | ph | f sound |
| t | th | soft th |
| b | bh | v sound |
| m | mh | v sound |
| g | gh | silent/soft |
| d | dh | soft th |
| s | s | unchanged |
| f | fh | silent |

### Vowel Shifts Applied

| Original | Mutated |
|----------|---------|
| é | èi or éi |
| ú | ùi or úi |
| á | à |
| ö | ò |
| ü | ùi |

## Line Names

Inherited family names. These work differently by region.

### Heartlands: Patronymic System

Line names use ancestor's Given Name + relationship suffix.

**Relationship Suffixes:**
- *-fí* (son of) → after mutation: **-fhì** (silent f, sounds like "-ee")
- *-ín* (daughter of) → after mutation: **-ìn**
- *-nók* (descended from) → after mutation: **-nòc** (gender-neutral, for distant ancestors)

#### Construction Example

If ancestor was named **Còcal** (stone-heart):

| Descendant | Construction | Result | Pronunciation |
|------------|--------------|--------|---------------|
| Son | Còcal + fí | Còcalfhì | KOH-kal-ee |
| Daughter | Còcal + ín | Còcalìn | KOH-kal-een |
| Distant | Còcal + nók | Còcalnòc | KOH-kal-nohk |

### Vent Lands: Guild-Ancestor System

Line names combine Guild identifier + founding ancestor.

**Guild Prefixes:**
- *Vas-* (Iron Guild / Smiths)
- *Còt-* (Binding Guild)
- *Szòv-* (Weaving Guild)
- *Hàj-* (Ship Guild)

#### Construction Example

Smith guild, ancestor **Eròcal**:

| Step | Process | Result |
|------|---------|--------|
| Guild | Vas- (iron) | Vas |
| Ancestor | Eròcal | Eròcal |
| Combined | | **Vas-Eròcal** |
| Pronunciation | | VAHSH-eh-roh-kal |

### Shattered Reaches: Ship/Island + Deed System

Line names use vessel or island name + notable deed.

**Common Deed Suffixes:**
- *-tòrò* (from törő, breaker) — for those who broke something significant
- *-mentò* (from mentő, saver) — for those who saved something/someone
- *-jàrò* (from járó, walker/traveler) — for explorers
- *-làtò* (from látó, seer) — for those with notable foresight

#### Construction Example

Ship "Vìztar" (water-path), deed of breaking a blockade:

| Step | Process | Result |
|------|---------|--------|
| Ship | Vìztar | Vìztar |
| Deed | törő → tòrò | tòrò |
| Combined | | **Vìztar-tòrò** |
| Pronunciation | | VIZ-tar-TOH-roh |

## Regional Naming Conventions

### Heartlands (Fonváros)

**Full Formal Address** (legal, major ceremonies):
> Given + Virtue + Fate + Craft + Line

Example: *Fèinnav Ìghazàhd Ùtcai Cèrescrav Còcalfhì*
"Light-self, truth-covenanted, road-bound, merchant-bonded, son of stone-heart"

**Standard Formal** (most official contexts):
> Given + Fate + Line

Example: *Fèinnav Ùtcai Còcalfhì*

**Common Address** (daily among privileged):
> Given + Line

Example: *Fèinnav Còcalfhì*

**Constrained Address** (workers, poor):
Often lack Line names. Use Given + Craft or Given alone.

Example: *Àrnnav Còtòcai* or just *Àrnnav*

### Vent Lands (Öntőváros)

**Full Formal Address**:
> Guild-Line + Craft + Given + Fate

Example: *Vas-Eròcal Còvàcscal Tùizcal Vàscai*
"Smith-guild of strength-heart, smith-hearted, fire-heart, iron-bound"

**Working Address** (most common):
> Craft + Given

Example: *Còvàcscal Tùizcal*

**Guild Address** (within guild):
> Guild-Line + Given

Example: *Vas-Eròcal Tùizcal*

### Shattered Reaches (Szigetköz)

**Full Formal Address** (rare, legal only):
> Ship/Island-Deed + Given + Fate

Example: *Vìztar-tòrò Èitar Vèszttar*
"Water-path breaker, night-path, maze-walker"

**Common Address**:
> Given + Ship/Island

Example: *Èitar Vìztar*

**Intimate/Crew Address**:
> Nickname based on deed or characteristic (not formal name)

Fate Names considered private in the Reaches—asking someone's Fate Name is an intimate question.

## Naming Customs

### Birth Naming
Parents choose Given Names based on hopes for the child. Common to consult with a Loom-Reader for "favorable" sounds, though this service costs extra.

### Virtue Naming (Age 7)
Community ceremony where elders observe children and assign Virtue Names based on demonstrated character. Children may receive unflattering Virtue Names if they've behaved poorly—these follow them for life.

### Fate Naming (Binding at 15)
The Loom-Reader assigns the Fate Name based on the Sórscad reading. This is the most significant name for legal and social purposes in the Heartlands.

### Craft Naming
Earned upon completing apprenticeship or demonstrating professional competence. Some people earn multiple Craft Names over a lifetime.

### Line Naming
Inherited, but can be revoked (becoming "Lineless" or *Vonalnòc* — "line-empty") for serious crimes or betrayals. Being Lineless is a severe social stigma.
