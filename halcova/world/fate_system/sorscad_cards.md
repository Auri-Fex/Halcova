# The Complete Sórscad: All Fifty Cards

## Overview

Each of the five circles contains ten archetypal cards. This document provides the complete set with meanings, associations, and implications.

---

## Circle One: Léilcrù (Core/Soul)

The Léilcrù cards describe essential nature—who you ARE at the deepest level.

### 1. The Lantern (Lámpás)

**Essence**: Inner light. Self-knowledge. Introspection.

**Character Type**: Those who illuminate their own depths. Philosophers, contemplatives, those who think before acting.

**Positive Reading**: Clarity, self-awareness, wisdom, inner peace.

**Negative Reading**: Paralysis through over-analysis, disconnection from others, solipsism.

**Pattern Implications**: High stability, moderate predictability, low deviation rate. The Loom reads Lanterns as steady.

---

### 2. The Mask (Álarc)

**Essence**: Performance. Adaptation. The constructed self.

**Character Type**: Those who become what situations require. Actors, diplomats, survivors who learned to hide.

**Positive Reading**: Flexibility, social intelligence, protection of inner self.

**Negative Reading**: Loss of authentic identity, manipulation, inability to connect genuinely.

**Pattern Implications**: Variable readings—Masks are harder to predict because their presented self isn't their true self.

---

### 3. The Mirror (Tükör)

**Essence**: Reflection. Identity through others. The observed self.

**Character Type**: Those who understand themselves through relationships. Empaths, caregivers, those who absorb others' expectations.

**Positive Reading**: Deep empathy, social connection, understanding of others.

**Negative Reading**: Loss of self, dependency, inability to function alone.

**Pattern Implications**: Readings influenced by proximity to others. Mirror individuals in isolation read differently than in company.

---

### 4. The Anchor (Horgony)

**Essence**: Stability. Unchanging core. The immovable self.

**Character Type**: Those who remain themselves regardless of circumstance. Traditionalists, the stubborn, the unshakeable.

**Positive Reading**: Reliability, strength, consistency, trustworthiness.

**Negative Reading**: Rigidity, inability to grow, brittleness when forced to change.

**Pattern Implications**: Extremely high predictability. Anchors rarely deviate—but when they do, it's catastrophic.

---

### 5. The Crown (Korona)

**Essence**: Authority. Self-sovereignty. The commanding self.

**Character Type**: Those who naturally lead. Authority figures, the confident, those who expect obedience.

**Positive Reading**: Leadership, decisiveness, natural authority, vision.

**Negative Reading**: Arrogance, tyranny, inability to follow, isolation at the top.

**Pattern Implications**: High visibility in Loom readings. Crowns attract prediction attention.

---

### 6. The Wound (Seb)

**Essence**: Damage. Defensive formation. The scarred self.

**Character Type**: Those shaped by trauma. Survivors, the damaged, those who protect a core injury.

**Positive Reading**: Resilience, hard-won wisdom, empathy for suffering.

**Negative Reading**: Defensiveness, inability to trust, projection of pain onto others.

**Pattern Implications**: Unpredictable—Wounds respond to threat-perception, not objective circumstance.

---

### 7. The Husk (Héj)

**Essence**: Emptiness. The hollowed self. What remains when something was removed.

**Character Type**: Those who've lost something essential. The grieving, the burned-out, those who survive but don't live.

**Positive Reading**: Capacity for transformation, freedom from attachment, potential for renewal.

**Negative Reading**: Emptiness, depression, inability to feel, drift toward non-existence.

**Pattern Implications**: Low pattern-weight. Husks are less visible to the Loom, which some exploit deliberately.

---

### 8. The Echo (Visszhang)

**Essence**: Inheritance. The carried self. Identity shaped by ancestry.

**Character Type**: Those defined by family, tradition, inherited role. Heirs, those living in ancestors' shadows.

**Positive Reading**: Connection to lineage, inherited wisdom, continuity with past.

**Negative Reading**: Inability to be oneself, trapped by others' expectations, haunted by ancestry.

**Pattern Implications**: Readings influenced by family pattern-history. Echoes are partially predictable through lineage analysis.

---

### 9. The Shard (Szilánk)

**Essence**: Fragmentation. The broken self. Multiple identities not unified.

**Character Type**: Those with dissociated or compartmentalized selves. The traumatized, the complex, those who are different people in different contexts.

**Positive Reading**: Complexity, adaptability, survival through division.

**Negative Reading**: Fragmentation, internal conflict, lack of coherent identity.

**Pattern Implications**: Difficult readings—Shards produce inconsistent data. Some Loom-Keepers refuse to read them.

---

### 10. The Spark (Szikra)

**Essence**: Emergence. The becoming self. Identity in formation.

**Character Type**: Those still discovering who they are. The young, the transforming, those in crisis of identity.

**Positive Reading**: Potential, growth, openness, possibility.

**Negative Reading**: Instability, inability to commit, perpetual adolescence.

**Pattern Implications**: High variance. Sparks are the hardest to predict because they're still forming.

---

## Circle Two: Húslaimh (Flesh/Body)

The Húslaimh cards describe physical nature—how you exist in material reality.

### 1. Iron (Vas)

**Essence**: Strength. Durability. The body as fortress.

**Character Type**: The physically powerful. Warriors, laborers, those who rely on their bodies.

**Associations**: Military service, physical labor, endurance, violence.

---

### 2. Stone (Kő)

**Essence**: Endurance. Patience. The body as monument.

**Character Type**: Those who persist. Long-livers, the stubborn, those who outlast rather than overpower.

**Associations**: Long life, slow metabolism, resistance to illness, immobility.

---

### 3. Silk (Selyem)

**Essence**: Sensitivity. Refinement. The body as instrument.

**Character Type**: Those with heightened physical awareness. Artists, sensualists, those who feel everything.

**Associations**: Art, pleasure, sensitivity to environment, fragility.

---

### 4. Glass (Üveg)

**Essence**: Fragility. Transparency. The body as window.

**Character Type**: Those with delicate constitutions. The sickly, the sensitive, those whose health is precarious.

**Associations**: Illness, sensitivity, transparency of emotion, beauty and breakability.

---

### 5. Clay (Agyag)

**Essence**: Malleability. Potential. The body as material.

**Character Type**: Those who can be shaped. Adaptable physically, changeable, responsive to environment.

**Associations**: Physical adaptability, responsiveness to training, susceptibility to influence.

---

### 6. Flame (Láng)

**Essence**: Consumption. Energy. The body as furnace.

**Character Type**: Those who burn through life. High-energy individuals, those who consume and are consumed.

**Associations**: Passion, intensity, short life, metabolic extremes.

---

### 7. Water (Víz)

**Essence**: Flow. Adaptation. The body as current.

**Character Type**: Those who move through life fluidly. Adaptable, changeable, finding the path of least resistance.

**Associations**: Flexibility, fertility, emotional connection to physicality.

---

### 8. Bone (Csont)

**Essence**: Structure. Foundation. The body as framework.

**Character Type**: Those defined by their skeleton. Lean, structured, fundamental rather than ornamental.

**Associations**: Asceticism, structural integrity, reduction to essentials.

---

### 9. Root (Gyökér)

**Essence**: Connection. Grounding. The body as anchor to place.

**Character Type**: Those tied to location. Farmers, place-bound individuals, those who sicken away from home.

**Associations**: Connection to land, immobility, drawing sustenance from environment.

---

### 10. Seed (Mag)

**Essence**: Potential. Reproduction. The body as vessel for future.

**Character Type**: Those whose physical purpose is continuation. Parents, those focused on legacy.

**Associations**: Fertility, inheritance, investment in future generations.

---

## Circle Three: Cóitcai (Binding/Connection)

The Cóitcai cards describe relationship patterns—how you connect to others and obligations.

### 1. Pact (Egyezség)

**Essence**: Contract. Formal agreement. Relationships as transactions.

**Character Type**: Those who formalize connections. Professionals, those who prefer clear terms.

**Associations**: Business relationships, formal agreements, clarity of obligation.

---

### 2. Chain (Lánc)

**Essence**: Bondage. Obligation. Relationships as constraint.

**Character Type**: Those bound by duty. Servants, the obligated, those who cannot leave.

**Associations**: Duty, constraint, inherited obligation, inescapable connection.

---

### 3. Web (Háló)

**Essence**: Network. Multiple connections. Relationships as system.

**Character Type**: Those embedded in complex social networks. Networkers, community members.

**Associations**: Social intelligence, multiple connections, community membership.

---

### 4. Hearth (Tűzhely)

**Essence**: Intimacy. Home. Relationships as warmth.

**Character Type**: Those focused on close bonds. Family-oriented, homebodies, domestic focus.

**Associations**: Family, close friendships, warmth, domestic life.

---

### 5. Blade (Penge)

**Essence**: Opposition. Conflict. Relationships as combat.

**Character Type**: Those defined by enemies. Rivals, the contentious, those who connect through conflict.

**Associations**: Rivalry, competition, definition through opposition.

---

### 6. Thorn (Tövis)

**Essence**: Pain. Difficult connection. Relationships that wound.

**Character Type**: Those whose connections hurt. Abusive relationships, difficult attachments.

**Associations**: Painful bonds, inability to leave harmful connections, love that damages.

---

### 7. Twin (Iker)

**Essence**: Mirroring. Deep pairing. Relationships as reflection.

**Character Type**: Those defined by a single other. Partners, twins, those in consuming dyads.

**Associations**: Deep partnership, merging identities, co-dependency.

---

### 8. Flock (Nyáj)

**Essence**: Collectivity. Group membership. Relationships as belonging.

**Character Type**: Those who find identity in groups. Team players, followers, those who need belonging.

**Associations**: Group membership, conformity, finding self in collective.

---

### 9. Ward (Védelem)

**Essence**: Protection. Guardian relationships. Caring for others.

**Character Type**: Those who protect. Parents, guardians, those who define themselves through care.

**Associations**: Protection, caregiving, responsibility for others.

---

### 10. Veil (Fátyol)

**Essence**: Concealment. Hidden connections. Secret relationships.

**Character Type**: Those with hidden bonds. Secret lovers, spies, those whose connections are concealed.

**Associations**: Secret relationships, hidden loyalties, concealed connections.

---

## Circle Four: Hálmaht (Death/End)

The Hálmaht cards describe mortality relationship—how you face endings.

### 1. Threshold (Küszöb)

**Essence**: Transition. Death as passage. Endings as doorways.

**Character Type**: Those who die in transformation. Deaths during change, liminal deaths.

**Associations**: Violent death during transition, death as transformation, significance in ending.

---

### 2. Rest (Nyugalom)

**Essence**: Peace. Death as sleep. Endings as completion.

**Character Type**: Those who die peacefully. Natural deaths, accepted endings, completed lives.

**Associations**: Peaceful death, old age, acceptance, completion.

---

### 3. Blade (Penge)

**Essence**: Violence. Death as cutting. Endings through force.

**Character Type**: Those who die violently. Murder victims, warriors, those cut down.

**Associations**: Violent death, murder, warfare, sudden ending.

---

### 4. Fade (Halványulás)

**Essence**: Gradual diminishment. Death as fading. Endings through attrition.

**Character Type**: Those who fade away. Illness, slow decline, forgotten deaths.

**Associations**: Long illness, being forgotten, gradual irrelevance, quiet ending.

---

### 5. Hunger (Éhség)

**Essence**: Consumption. Death as feeding. Endings that nourish something else.

**Character Type**: Those whose deaths serve others. Sacrifices, those consumed by larger forces.

**Associations**: Sacrifice, being consumed, death that serves purpose, feeding larger systems.

---

### 6. Storm (Vihar)

**Essence**: Catastrophe. Death as disaster. Endings in chaos.

**Character Type**: Those who die in catastrophes. Disaster victims, collateral damage.

**Associations**: Disaster death, accident, being caught in larger events, random ending.

---

### 7. Ice (Jég)

**Essence**: Preservation. Death as freezing. Endings that preserve.

**Character Type**: Those whose deaths preserve something. Deaths that freeze moments, memorialized endings.

**Associations**: Preserved death, becoming monument, frozen in memory.

---

### 8. Fire (Tűz)

**Essence**: Transformation. Death as burning. Endings that change everything.

**Character Type**: Those whose deaths transform. Martyrs, catalysts, deaths that ignite change.

**Associations**: Transformative death, martyrdom, death that changes others.

---

### 9. Water (Víz)

**Essence**: Dissolution. Death as drowning. Endings that dissolve boundaries.

**Character Type**: Those who dissolve. Drowning deaths, merging with something larger.

**Associations**: Drowning, dissolution of self, merging with larger waters.

---

### 10. Void (Űr)

**Essence**: Emptiness. Death as absence. Endings into nothing.

**Character Type**: Those who leave nothing behind. Forgotten deaths, unmourned endings.

**Associations**: Being forgotten, leaving no trace, death without meaning.

---

## Circle Five: Sórscad (Fate/Path)

The Sórscad cards describe trajectory—how you move through life.

### 1. Road (Út)

**Essence**: Linearity. Direct progress. Clear path forward.

**Character Type**: Those on clear trajectories. Ambitious, goal-oriented, knowing where they're going.

**Associations**: Career advancement, clear goals, predictable progression.

---

### 2. Ladder (Létra)

**Essence**: Hierarchy. Vertical movement. Climbing or falling.

**Character Type**: Those focused on position. Status-seekers, those measuring themselves against others.

**Associations**: Social climbing, status consciousness, vertical mobility.

---

### 3. Crossroads (Keresztút)

**Essence**: Choice. Pivots. Moments of decision.

**Character Type**: Those who face major choices. Decision-makers, those at turning points.

**Associations**: Major decisions, life pivots, choice-defined trajectory.

---

### 4. Maze (Labirintus)

**Essence**: Complexity. Confusion. Convoluted paths.

**Character Type**: Those whose paths twist. Confused, lost, finding way through complication.

**Associations**: Confused trajectory, lost periods, complexity of path.

---

### 5. Spiral (Spirál)

**Essence**: Return. Cycles. Coming back to familiar ground.

**Character Type**: Those who cycle through patterns. Repeaters, those who revisit, cyclical lives.

**Associations**: Repetition, returning to old patterns, cyclical existence.

---

### 6. Bridge (Híd)

**Essence**: Connection. Crossing. Paths that link.

**Character Type**: Those who connect others. Intermediaries, translators, those who span divides.

**Associations**: Connecting different worlds, bridging gaps, facilitating others' journeys.

---

### 7. Hunt (Vadászat)

**Essence**: Pursuit. Seeking. Paths defined by prey.

**Character Type**: Those pursuing something. Seekers, hunters, those defined by what they chase.

**Associations**: Goal pursuit, hunting, trajectory defined by target.

---

### 8. Drift (Sodródás)

**Essence**: Passivity. Carried by currents. Path not chosen.

**Character Type**: Those moved by external forces. Passive, responsive, going where pushed.

**Associations**: Passive trajectory, being carried, not choosing path.

---

### 9. Orbit (Pálya)

**Essence**: Circling. Bound paths. Movement around something fixed.

**Character Type**: Those circling something. Devoted, focused on a center, paths defined by what they circle.

**Associations**: Orbiting others/goals, devoted pursuit, circular path.

---

### 10. Break (Törés)

**Essence**: Rupture. Discontinuity. Paths that shatter.

**Character Type**: Those whose paths break. Revolutionaries, those who shatter expectations, discontinuous lives.

**Associations**: Breaking from expected path, discontinuity, revolutionary trajectory.

---

## Pattern Family Examples

### Named Families (Cultural Recognition)

**"Steadfasts"** (Lantern-Iron-Pact-Rest-Road)
- Reliable, predictable, institutional
- Common in Heartlands middle class
- Valued by employers and lenders

**"Knot-Born"** (Wound-Stone-Thorn-Blade-Maze)
- Troubled, resilient, difficult
- Common in pattern-constrained communities
- Distrusted by institutions; valued by survivors

**"Storm-Touched"** (Spark-Glass-Twin-Threshold-Break)
- Brilliant, unstable, transformative
- Rare but memorable
- Romanticized in stories; feared in institutions

**"Hollow Ones"** (Husk-Bone-Chain-Void-Drift)
- Empty, obligated, fading
- Often pattern-constrained
- The Absent are attracted to them

**"Crown-Bearers"** (Crown-Iron-Ward-Fire-Road)
- Leaders, authorities, visionaries
- Common in ruling families
- Expected to lead; pressured from birth

**"Woven"** (Echo-Root-Flock-Ice-Orbit)
- Community-embedded, traditional
- Common in rural areas
- Valued for stability; constrained by expectation

**"Blade-Dancers"** (Spark-Flame-Blade-Blade-Hunt)
- Violent, intense, pursuing
- Common in military, enforcement
- Wardens often have this pattern

**"Silk-Veiled"** (Mirror-Silk-Veil-Fade-Spiral)
- Hidden, sensitive, cyclical
- Common in court intrigue
- Valued for subtlety; distrusted for secrecy

## Reading Interactions

### Circle Combinations

Certain combinations are notable:

**Léilcrù Lantern + Sórscad Maze**: Clear inner vision, confused external path. The contemplative lost.

**Húslaimh Flame + Hálmaht Rest**: Intense life, peaceful death. Burns bright, ends quietly.

**Cóitcai Chain + Sórscad Break**: Bound to obligation, destined to rupture. Revolution against duty.

**Léilcrù Crown + Cóitcai Flock**: Born to lead, drawn to follow. Internal conflict between authority and belonging.

**Húslaimh Glass + Hálmaht Blade**: Fragile body, violent death. The vulnerability that invites predators.
