# Government and Political Structures

## The Heartlands Crown

### The Monarchy

**Current Status** (Year 476):
- The Crown is the nominal head of the Heartlands
- Current monarch: aging, succession uncertain
- Real power distributed among Crown, nobles, Concordat, and guilds

**Royal Authority**:
- Theoretical: All land held from Crown, all justice derived from Crown
- Practical: Power shared with nobles, Church, and Concordat
- Can issue edicts, but implementation requires cooperation
- Commands the Crown Army directly
- Appoints major officials

**Succession**:
- Primogeniture (eldest son inherits)
- Pattern-reading of heirs is customary (not legally required)
- Current uncertainty: No clear heir, multiple claimants
- Concordat involvement in succession is controversial

### The Noble Council

**Composition**:
- Great Lords: ~20 major noble houses
- Lesser Lords: Representatives from ~100 minor houses
- Church representatives (non-voting observers)
- Concordat representatives (non-voting observers)

**Powers**:
- Advise the Crown (required for major decisions)
- Approve taxation (theoretical veto)
- Elect regent if monarch incapacitated
- Can petition for Crown judgment against other nobles

**Meetings**:
- Full council: Twice yearly (Láng and Hanyatlás)
- Emergency sessions as needed
- Location: The capital, Fonváros

### Royal Administration

**The Chancellery**:
- Chief minister (Chancellor) runs daily government
- Record-keeping, correspondence, coordination
- Staffed by pattern-privileged bureaucrats
- Significant independent power

**The Treasury**:
- Tax collection and disbursement
- Crown lands management
- Mint operations
- Chronic deficit management

**The Judiciary**:
- Royal courts system
- Appointed judges
- Appeals from noble courts

**The Military**:
- Crown Army command structure
- Fortification maintenance
- Border defense coordination

## Noble Houses

### Structure of Nobility

**Great Houses** (~20):
- Control major territories (counties, duchies)
- Seats on Noble Council
- Private armies (1,000-5,000 soldiers)
- Marriage alliances with Crown
- Pattern-privileged by definition

**Lesser Houses** (~100):
- Control smaller territories (baronies, manors)
- Council representation through Great House patrons
- Smaller military forces (100-1,000)
- Dependent on Great House alliances

**Knights and Minor Gentry** (~1,000):
- Individual estates or positions
- No Council representation
- Military service obligations
- Boundary between minor gentry and wealthy commoners blurs

### Noble Powers

**Within Their Territories**:
- Collect taxes (portion to Crown)
- Administer justice (except pattern-crimes)
- Raise military levies
- Appoint local officials
- Regulate trade (within limits)

**Limitations**:
- Cannot coin money
- Cannot try pattern-crimes (Concordat jurisdiction)
- Military force limited by treaty
- Crown can revoke grants (rarely, for cause)

### Current Great Houses (Examples)

**House Vaskút** ("Iron Spring"):
- Controls eastern Heartlands
- Mining and metalwork wealth
- Current head: Lord Béla Vaskút, elderly, three sons competing
- Close ties to Vent Lands guilds

**House Aranypatak** ("Gold Stream"):
- Controls Gold River valley, including capital approaches
- Agricultural wealth, strategic position
- Current head: Lady Erzsébet Aranypatak, middle-aged, capable
- Crown loyalist faction leader

**House Hidegvár** ("Cold Castle"):
- Controls northern frontier
- Military tradition, Warden Order ties
- Current head: Lord Miklós Hidegvár, young, aggressive
- Ambitious, possible succession claimant

## The Concordat

### Structure

**The Synod**:
- Governing body of the Concordat
- ~30 senior Loom-Keepers
- Meets quarterly at Great Node
- Elects the High Weaver

**The High Weaver**:
- Head of the Concordat
- Speaks for the Loom in political matters
- 10-year term, can be re-elected
- Significant political influence

**Regional Hierarchs**:
- Oversee Loom operations by region
- Coordinate with secular authorities
- Report to Synod

**Loom-Keepers**:
- Operate pattern-engines
- Perform readings
- Maintain Registry
- ~5,000 across all regions

### Concordat Powers

**Exclusive Jurisdiction**:
- Pattern-crime prosecution
- Loom operation and maintenance
- Registry management
- Loom-Keeper training and certification

**Shared Jurisdiction**:
- Pattern-reading for courts (advisory)
- Religious interpretation (with Church)
- Practitioner regulation (with Warden Order)

**Political Influence**:
- Pattern-readings affect everything
- Information advantage (they see patterns)
- Economic power (Residue income)
- Can influence succession through pattern-assessment

### Relationship with Crown

**Formal**: Independent institution, neither above nor below Crown
**Practical**: Mutual dependence
- Crown needs Loom for governance
- Concordat needs Crown for enforcement and protection
- Tension over jurisdiction and influence
- Current balance: Uneasy cooperation

## The Warden Order

### Organizational Structure

**The Grand Warden**:
- Overall commander
- Based at headquarters in Fonváros
- Reports to Synod (theoretically), independent (practically)
- Current: Grand Warden Софія Vasfal, ruthless, effective

**Regional Commanders**:
- One per major region
- Significant autonomy
- Coordinate with local authorities
- Command 500-1,000 Wardens each

**Chapter Houses**:
- Local bases throughout Hálcova
- 20-50 Wardens each
- Investigation and enforcement
- Pattern-correction facilities attached

**Field Wardens**:
- Operational personnel
- Investigators, enforcers, hunters
- The Attuned (enhanced capabilities)
- Total: ~5,000 active Wardens

### Warden Powers

**Can Do**:
- Investigate pattern-crimes anywhere
- Detain suspects without warrant
- Conduct pattern-readings without consent
- Enter premises searching for practitioners
- Execute pattern-criminals (with tribunal approval)
- Override secular authority on pattern matters

**Cannot Do**:
- Try non-pattern crimes
- Interfere in purely secular matters
- Be commanded by secular authorities
- Operate without Concordat oversight (theoretically)

### The Attunement

**What It Is**:
- Ritual process binding Warden to Loom
- Enhances pattern-perception
- Provides resistance to pattern-manipulation
- Creates physical/psychological changes

**What It Costs**:
- Shortened lifespan (average: 15-20 years post-Attunement)
- Pattern-lock (cannot deviate from own pattern)
- Psychological effects (doubt causes pain)
- Cannot leave the Order

**Who Undergoes It**:
- Voluntary (officially)
- ~30% of Wardens are Attuned
- The Attuned are the elite hunters
- Non-Attuned handle administration, basic enforcement

### Fate Hounds

**What They Are**:
- Wolves modified through pattern-attunement
- Track patterns across any distance
- Sense deviation before it manifests
- ~200 exist across all regions

**How They're Used**:
- Tracking fugitive practitioners
- Detecting infiltrators
- Disrupting enemy pattern-abilities
- Terror/intimidation

**Limitations**:
- Expensive to maintain
- Cannot be fully controlled
- May turn on handlers
- Breeding program has limited success

## City Governance

### Major Cities

**Fonváros** (The Capital):
- Population: ~150,000
- Seat of Crown, Concordat headquarters, Great Node
- Governed by Royal Governor + City Council
- Multiple jurisdictions overlap

**Other Major Cities** (50,000-100,000):
- Regional capitals
- Noble seat or major trade hub
- Governor appointed by Crown or local lord
- Guild influence significant

### City Government Structure

**Governor/Mayor**:
- Appointed (by Crown, lord, or elected depending on charter)
- Executive authority
- Coordinates with guilds, Church, Wardens

**City Council**:
- Guild representatives
- Wealthy merchants
- Sometimes neighborhood representatives
- Advisory to executive, sometimes more

**City Guard**:
- Police function
- Under governor's authority
- Pattern-checking at gates
- Coordination with Wardens on pattern-matters

### Urban Jurisdictions

**Overlapping Authority**:
- Crown/noble law for secular crimes
- Concordat for pattern-crimes
- Church for religious matters
- Guild courts for trade disputes
- Creates conflict and confusion

**The Warden Problem**:
- Wardens can override city authority
- City guards resent this
- Wealthy can sometimes avoid Warden attention
- Poor cannot

## Regional Political Systems

### The Shattered Reaches

**No Central Government**:
- Confederation of islands and city-states
- The Reaches League (loose alliance)
- No single authority over all

**Island Governance**:
- Varies by island
- Hereditary chiefs, elected councils, merchant oligarchies
- Some islands recognize Heartlands sovereignty (nominally)
- Most are effectively independent

**The Reaches League**:
- Mutual defense pact
- Trade coordination
- Meets irregularly
- No enforcement mechanism
- Heartlands influence through trade and diplomacy

### The Middle Waters

**Colonial Administration**:
- Multiple colonial powers present
- No unified governance
- Indigenous authorities persist in some areas
- Constant jurisdictional conflict

**The Great Island**:
- Nominally Heartlands territory
- Practically run by colonial companies
- Governor appointed but weak
- Real power: Company directors, indigenous chiefs, Colony Cults

### The Vent Lands

**Guild Federation**:
- Major guilds share governance
- Guild Council as ruling body
- No hereditary nobility (in theory)
- Wealth and skill determine status

**The Guild Council**:
- Representatives from major guilds
- Rotating leadership
- Consensus decision-making (slow)
- Military coordination when needed

**Relationship with Heartlands**:
- Officially independent
- Trade relationships crucial
- Concordat presence tolerated
- Warden Order limited authority

### The Far Shelf

**No Central Authority**:
- Clan-based governance
- Indigenous confederacies
- Colonial trading posts (Heartlands-backed)
- Hollow Saint communities

**Clan Structure**:
- Hereditary chiefs with council of elders
- Decisions by consensus
- Inter-clan disputes settled by tradition or force
- No written law

**Colonial Presence**:
- Trading posts along coast
- Mining operations (contested)
- Nominal Heartlands authority
- Actual control limited to immediate vicinity

## Current Political Tensions (Year 476)

### The Succession Crisis

**The Problem**:
- Current monarch is aging, ill
- No clear heir (children died, disputed legitimacy)
- Multiple claimants from Great Houses
- Concordat involvement controversial

**The Claimants**:
- Lord Miklós Hidegvár (northern houses backing)
- Lady Erzsébet Aranypatak (Crown loyalist faction)
- Prince Tamás (distant cousin, Church backing)
- Others with weaker claims

**The Concordat Position**:
- Officially neutral
- Pattern-readings of claimants requested
- Whoever they favor gains advantage
- Rumors of manipulation

### Far Shelf Resistance

**The Situation**:
- Colonial extraction increasingly resisted
- Multiple clans in effective revolt
- Mining operations disrupted
- Violence increasing

**The Players**:
- Colonial companies (want extraction to continue)
- Indigenous confederacies (want independence)
- Hollow Saints (ideological support for resistance)
- Crown (wants stability and resources)

### Middle Waters Instability

**The Situation**:
- Colonial powers competing
- Indigenous authorities caught between
- Colony Cult influence growing
- Volcanic activity increasing (natural or otherwise?)

**The Players**:
- Heartlands colonial companies
- Reaches trading interests
- Indigenous chiefs
- Colony Cults
- Vent Lands guild interests

### Hollow Saint Activity

**The Situation**:
- Underground movement becoming bolder
- Attacks on Warden Order increasing
- Theological challenge to Orthodox/Concordat
- Far Shelf provides sanctuary

**The Response**:
- Warden Order intensified pursuit
- Church condemnation
- Some sympathy among pattern-constrained
- Risk of overreaction creating martyrs

## Summary

Political power in Hálcova is distributed among:

- **The Crown**: Nominal head, real power limited by others
- **Noble Houses**: Territorial control, military force, Council influence
- **The Concordat**: Pattern-control, information advantage, economic power
- **The Warden Order**: Enforcement monopoly on pattern-crimes
- **Guilds**: Economic power, especially in Vent Lands
- **Regional authorities**: Varying systems in Reaches, Middle Waters, Far Shelf

Current tensions include succession uncertainty, colonial resistance, and Hollow Saint challenge to the established order.
