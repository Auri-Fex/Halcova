Hálcova: Geography and Continental Structure

See also World Summary, Metaphysics, Magic Systems, Fate System, Society, Governance, and Conlang.

Overview

Hálcova's landmasses are arranged around the habitable equatorial band, with two major continents and three significant landmasses forming five distinct regions. The geography tells a story—the Heartlands and Shattered Reaches were once a single continent, torn apart by some ancient catastrophe. The rift that separated them became the Narrow Sea.

The Shattered Heartlands Theory

The Evidence

Looking at the coastlines, the relationship is obvious: - The ragged eastern coast of the Greater Continent matches the western edge of the Shattered Reaches - Rock formations, mineral deposits, and fossil-equivalents continue across the gap - The Narrow Sea is a flooded rift valley, not a true ocean basin - Ancient ruins exist on both sides with matching architectural styles

The Heartlands

Spine of the continent, river valleys, southern coast, northern edge. Orthodox faith, Loom integration, trade networks. Specific nations to be developed.

The Shattered Reaches

Fragmented island-continent, matching coast, interior highlands, fracture zone, island chains. Maritime character, city-states, merchant leagues.

The Middle Waters

Volcanic archipelago, caldera, reef systems, thermal vents. Warmest region, contested territory, colony cults, religious diversity.

The Vent Lands

Continental fragment, volcanic activity, fire range, thermal valleys, steam fields, sulfur flats. Geothermal independence, trade federation, fleshwright guilds.

The Far Shelf

Large, flat, cold continent, southern ice edge, mineral ranges, tundra. Frontier character, indigenous confederacies, religious heterodoxy.
