# Conlang Baseline: Grammar, Phonology, Orthography

This file expands the three-language, molting, and enunciation rules to cover broader aspects of the conlang.

---

## Phonology & Phonotactics
- **Sound Inventory:**
  - Consonants and vowels are drawn from Mongolian, Kiranti, and Fula languages.
  - Common sounds: k, g, t, d, s, sh, y, j, n, m, a, e, i, o, u
- **Syllable Structure:**
  - Prefer open syllables (CV), but allow CVC and CCV from Kiranti and Fula influence.
  - Avoid more than two consonants together.
- **Stress:**
  - Stress falls on the first syllable, unless molting creates a single syllable.

---

## Grammar & Syntax
- **Word Order:**
  - Default to SVO (Subject-Verb-Object), as in Mongolian and Fula.
- **Nouns:**
  - Pluralization by adding -k or -n (from Kiranti/Fula).
  - Possession by suffixing -in or -en.
- **Verbs:**
  - Present tense: root form
  - Past tense: add -ta/-da
  - Future tense: add -sha/-ja
- **Adjectives:**
  - Placed before nouns, no agreement required.

---

## Morphology
- **Compounding:**
  - Combine roots using molting and mutation rules, drop unstressed vowels.
- **Inflection:**
  - Use affix variations for tense, plurality, and possession.

---

## Orthography
- **Writing System:**
  - Use Latin script for accessibility.
  - Apostrophes and hyphens allowed for clarity, but molting tends to remove them.
- **Spelling Conventions:**
  - Spell words as pronounced after enunciation.
  - Avoid silent letters.

---

## Core Vocabulary Examples
- **Numbers:**
  - 1: ek
  - 2: du
  - 3: trin
  - 4: char
  - 5: pan
- **Kinship:**
  - Mother: mak
  - Father: fak
  - Child: nak
- **Elements:**
  - Water: ak
  - Fire: yak
  - Earth: dak
  - Air: shak
- **Colors:**
  - Red: rak
  - Blue: blak
  - Green: grak
  - White: shak
  - Black: nak

---

## Naming Conventions
- **Personal Names:**
  - Combine two roots, apply molting: makdak (Mother Earth), fakyak (Father Fire)
- **Place Names:**
  - Use core vocabulary and molting: akshak (Water Air, for a coastal city)
