## The Lost Ages (Unknown-1000)

### What We Don't Know

**The Gap**:
- Reliable written records only go back ~1,000 years
- Before that: oral traditions, myths, fragments
- Ruins suggest much older civilizations
- Dead zones may contain complete lost cities

**Theories**:
1. **Cyclical Collapse**: This has happened before; previous civilizations rose, over-extracted, died
2. **Single Ancient Civilization**: One great pre-collapse culture fell; we rebuilt from scraps
3. **Multiple Cultures**: Different societies at different times; not all connected
4. **Natural Development**: Humans evolved here normally; no dramatic prehistory

**Evidence**:
- Ruins in dead zones (can't be studied, too dangerous)
- Ancient texts reference "before times"
- Technology that shouldn't exist yet (discovered, not invented?)
- Genetic diversity suggests long presence

## Pre-Collapse Civilization

### The Ruins

**What's Found**:
- Massive structures in dead zones (seen from distance)
- Advanced architecture (beyond medieval capability)
- Devices and artifacts (many non-functional)
- Written languages (partially deciphered)

**Central Tavanor Dead Zone**:
- Buried city at center
- Was capital (evidence suggests)
- Predates current kingdoms by centuries
- Created or destroyed by Second Cascade? (Unclear)

**The Undercity** (beneath Kalen-Sheloth):
- Ancient intact structures
- Pre-dates current city by 500+ years
- Functional artifacts still found
- Suggests earlier advanced settlement

**Konor Ruins**:
- Entire dead zone littered with cities
- Some only 200 years old (recent collapse)
- Others ancient (1,000+ years)
- Pattern: civilizations rose, extracted, died

### The Seventh and Eighth Continents Theory

**The Claim**:
Ancient texts reference "seventh and eighth" continents

**Current Reality**:
Only five continents exist (six if you count fragments)

**Hypothesis**:
- Two entire continents consumed by dead zones
- Happened long ago (1,000+ years?)
- Complete extinction of populations
- Land masses still exist but uninhabitable

**Counter-Arguments**:
- May be metaphorical (spiritual realms?)
- Translation errors
- Exaggeration or myth
- Continental drift (geological, not magical)

**Implications If True**:
- This cycle is ancient
- Extinction has happened before
- Current crisis not unprecedented
- May be inevitable

**Search**:
- Some scholars seek proof
- Expeditions into oldest dead zones (fatal)
- Most dismiss as myth
- Debate ongoing

## Early Human Settlements (1000-1400)

### Late Medieval Period

**Technology Level**:
- Iron Age to early medieval
- Muscle, wind, water power
- No magic technology yet
- Sustainable extraction only

**Population**:
- ~2-3 million globally (estimated)
- Concentrated near creature habitats
- Nomadic and settled both
- Regional kingdoms emerging

**Creature Interaction**:
- Hunting for food and materials
- Small-scale oil extraction (medicinal)
- Religious significance (some worshiped)
- Sustainable equilibrium

**Geography**:
- All five continents inhabited
- Travel difficult but possible
- Regional isolation common
- Trade routes primitive

### Early Kingdoms

**Tavanor**:
- Academic traditions begin
- First universities (monasteries)
- Knowledge preservation
- Political unification starting

**Sulenor**:
- Volcanic forge culture
- Metalworking expertise
- Fire-creature rituals
- Religious centers

**Eshtor**:
- Forest tribes
- Mega-tree settlements
- Creature symbiosis
- Oral traditions

**Vashor**:
- Nomadic clans
- Ice survival mastery
- Isolation from others
- Hardy culture

**Konor**:
- Agricultural prosperity
- Large population
- Irrigation networks
- Peaceful era

## The Discovery Era Prelude (1400-1500)

### Building Momentum

**Population Growth**:
- 3-4 million globally
- Cities emerging
- Agriculture improving
- Trade expanding

**Technology**:
- Late medieval peak
- Sailing ships
- Better metalworking
- Early firearms (unreliable)

**Creature Knowledge**:
- Hunters know habitats
- Some breeding attempts
- Medicinal uses documented
- Still religious/mystical understanding

**Political Structure**:
- Regional kingdoms
- Baron system emerging
- Loose federations
- Frequent conflicts (small scale)

**The Breakthrough Approaching**:
- Explorers push into harsh regions
- Discovery of concentrated oils imminent
- 1500s will change everything
- Last era of equilibrium

## Lost Knowledge

### What Ancient Texts Suggest

**Warnings**:
- "Do not extract beyond regeneration"
- "The substrate depletes; guard it"
- "Entropy spreads when balance breaks"
- "We were warned; we didn't listen"

**Blueprints**:
- Advanced devices in ruins
- Some match current technology
- Others beyond understanding
- Suggesting knowledge lost and rediscovered

**Maps**:
- Continental outlines accurate
- Some show features now buried in dead zones
- Others show continents that don't exist
- Navigation aids or myths?

**Philosophy**:
- Debates about extraction ethics (ancient)
- Conservation arguments (pre-crisis)
- Warnings ignored (repeatedly)
- Pattern: each generation thinks "different this time"

## Religious and Mythological Foundations

### Creation Myths

**The Bleeding Heart**:
- Meshvalt moon is heart of dead god
- Eruptions = divine blood
- Magic = gift and curse
- Popular belief

**The Frozen Prison**:
- World is prison for ancient demons
- Magic = demon essence
- Extraction = freeing demons gradually
- Eshtor shamanic tradition

**The Cycle**:
- World dies and rebirths
- Current age just one of many
- Entropy = natural death phase
- Konor mystics

**The Sacrifice**:
- Gods gave magic at cost
- Overuse = betraying gift
- Dead zones = divine punishment
- Faith of Fire interpretation

### Cultural Memory

**Oral Traditions**:
- Stories of "warmer times"
- Tales of "green world" (not blue)
- Legends of "yellow sun" (not Blaysol)
- Dismissed as fantasy or symbolism

**Seasonal Myths**:
- High-magic season = blessing
- Low-magic season = punishment
- Lunar cycles sacred
- Aurora as divine messages

**Creature Worship**:
- Some cultures worship magical beasts
- Taboos against harvesting (ignored by barons)
- Shamanic traditions (Eshtor, Vashor)
- Underground in industrial regions
