# Maps & Regional Detail

**Genre**: Dark Fantasy / Arcane Punk / Hard R
**Focus**: Entropy zones, dangerous territories, resource distribution, travel hazards

---

## I. Continental Overview

### The Known World
**Name**: Norbela (the inhabited world; single supercontinent)
**Size**: ~12 million square kilometers (roughly Australia-sized)
**Climate**: Cold; iceworld; blue sun (Blaysol); average temp -5°C to 15°C
**Geography**: Varied (mountains, forests, deserts, tundra, volcanic zones, fungal jungles)
**Habitability**: 60% habitable; 40% entropy dead zones (expanding)

---

### Five Major Regions (Baron Territories)

#### 1. **Tavanor** (Northeast) - ~2.5M km²
**Ruling Barons**: Shalen-Mor (Kalen-Sheloth), Telvor-Shin (Crystal Caverns)
**Geography**: Temperate forests, crystal caverns, ancient ruins, arcane academies
**Climate**: Mild (-5°C to 10°C)
**Population**: 3 million (30% in Kalen-Sheloth)
**Economy**: Academia, arcane research, crystal mining, trade hub
**Entropy Level**: Moderate (20% dead zones); central Tavanor = total dead zone (Second Cascade site)
**Dangers**: Entropy surges, bandit raids, political intrigue

#### 2. **Sulenor** (Southeast) - ~2M km²
**Ruling Barons**: Koresh-Val (Forge-Cities), Soryn-Kesh (Badlands)
**Geography**: Volcanic mountains, lava fields, obsidian wastes, fire-creature habitats
**Climate**: Hot (-0°C to 20°C in volcanic zones)
**Population**: 2.5 million (mostly Korbal/slaves in mines)
**Economy**: Fire-creature extraction (35% of continent's supply), metalworking, weaponry
**Entropy Level**: High (30% dead zones); aggressive extraction accelerating
**Dangers**: Volcanic eruptions, lava flows, fire-creatures (hostile), brutal overseers

#### 3. **Eshtor** (West) - ~3M km²
**Ruling Barons**: Velasha-Nen (Vorn-Merath), Dreshak-Mor (Swamplands)
**Geography**: Fungal jungles, swamps, mega-trees (100m tall), creature-rich ecosystems
**Climate**: Humid (-5°C to 15°C)
**Population**: 2 million (dispersed; many in hidden communities)
**Economy**: Forest/fungal creatures (20% supply), lumber, alchemy ingredients
**Entropy Level**: Moderate (15% dead zones); conservation efforts slow spread
**Dangers**: Vornalen-Keepers (hostile to outsiders), swamp diseases, corrupted creatures, guerrilla warfare

#### 4. **Vashor** (North) - ~2.5M km²
**Ruling Barons**: Threnok-Gash (Frozen Wastes), Nethik-Vorn (Tundra Holdings)
**Geography**: Tundra, glaciers, ice caverns, frozen seas
**Climate**: Freezing (-30°C to -5°C)
**Population**: 500,000 (sparse; nomadic tribes + mining outposts)
**Economy**: Rare ice-creatures (10% supply but highest quality), furs, preserved foods
**Entropy Level**: Low (5% dead zones); cold slows entropy (theory)
**Dangers**: Extreme cold, blizzards, ice-creatures (massive), northern vanishings (Nethvor's ritual), isolation

#### 5. **Konor** (Southwest) - ~2M km²
**Ruling Baron**: Meshira-Tal (Shira-Nesh Frontier)
**Geography**: Shisolak deserts (entropy wastelands), ancient ruins, dead seas
**Climate**: Cold deserts (-10°C to 10°C); arid
**Population**: 200,000 (down from 750,000; refugee exodus)
**Economy**: Collapsed; salvage/archaeology only; imports everything
**Entropy Level**: Critical (60% dead zones); terminal decline
**Dangers**: Entropy storms, corrupted creatures (aggressive), desperate refugees turned bandits, imminent weapon test

## II. Major Cities & Landmarks

### Tavanor Cities

#### **Kalen-Sheloth** (Capital; "Bright Hold")
- **Population**: 900,000 (largest city)
- **Location**: Central Tavanor; 50km from Second Cascade dead zone
- **Features**: 
  - Shalen-Mor's palace (arcane fortress; 200 rooms)
  - Arcane Academy (1,000 students; elite institution)
  - Sky Beacon (lighthouse-like structure; arcane light visible 100km; navigation/symbol of hope)
  - Council Hall (where 9 Barons meet)
  - Slums (400,000 in tenements; overcrowded; disease-ridden)
- **Economy**: Government, academia, trade, banking
- **Danger**: Political assassinations, Inquisitor presence, Breakers bombings
- **Travel**: Arcane rail hub; roads to all regions; 3-5 days to other capitals

#### **Telian-Kresh** (Secondary; "Sunlit Haven")
- **Population**: 150,000
- **Location**: Eastern Tavanor; coast
- **Features**: Port city; arcane shipyards; lighthouse; fishing industry
- **Economy**: Trade, fishing, shipbuilding
- **Danger**: Pirates, smugglers (Nethvor-controlled port), storms

#### **Gen-Sorath** (Tertiary; "Valley of Memories")
- **Population**: 80,000 (under siege; down from 100,000)
- **Location**: Southern Tavanor; mountain valley
- **Features**: 
  - Telumesh Archive (library; 1 million+ texts; under siege)
  - Crystal mines nearby
  - Arcane defenses (holding for now)
- **Economy**: Scholarship, mining, scribal work
- **Danger**: Inquisitor siege (1 month in); starvation imminent; assault expected

### Sulenor Cities

#### **Shira-Nesh** (Border with Konor; "Desert Entropy")
- **Population**: 50,000 (down from 200,000)
- **Location**: Sulenor/Konor border
- **Features**: Frontier town; salvagers; refugees; warlords
- **Economy**: Scavenging ruins; black market; human trafficking
- **Danger**: Entropy storms; bandits; Meshira-Tal's desperation; weapon test site if ultimatum fails
- **Travel**: 10 days from Vorn-Merath; dangerous route

## III. Travel Times & Routes

### By Foot/Horse
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Vorn-Merath**: 12 days
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Thren-Kalgash**: 20 days
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Meshira-Tal's Refuge**: 18 days
- **Vorn-Merath to First Grove**: Unknown (most die trying)

### By Arcane Carriage
- Speed: 30-50 km/h (depending on terrain)
- Range: 200km per shyak tank
- Cost: 500-1,000 GS per 100km
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Vorn-Merath**: 3 days
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Thren-Kalgash**: 8 days

### By Arcane Rail
- Speed: 80-100 km/h
- Routes: 5 major lines (Kalen-Sheloth hub)
- Cost: 10-50 GS per 100km (depending on class)
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Vorn-Merath**: 6 hours (when running; delays common)
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Thren-Kalgash**: No rail line (terrain impossible)

### By Skyship (Experimental; High Risk)
- Speed: 40-60 km/h
- Range: 500km per shyak tank
- Cost: 10,000+ GS per trip (military/desperate only)
- Failure Rate: 50% (crashes kill all)
- **Kalen-Sheloth to Vorn-Merath**: 8 hours (if doesn't crash)

## IV. Dangerous Zones & Travel Hazards

### Entropy Dead Zones (40% of Continent)

#### **Central Tavanor Dead Zone** (Second Cascade Site)
- **Size**: 500,000 km² (Texas-sized)
- **Entry**: Instant mutation; 100% fatality within hours
- **Features**: Nothing lives; air toxic; ground crystallized; ruins of ancient capital
- **Value**: Artifacts inside worth millions; no one survives retrieval
- **Bypass**: Travel routes detour; adds 5-10 days; necessary

#### **Konor Wastelands** (Shisolak Deserts)
- **Size**: 1.2M km² (60% of Konor)
- **Entry**: Slow death; days to weeks; mutations, madness, starvation
- **Features**: Ancient ruins; entropy storms; corrupted creatures
- **Value**: Meshira-Tal seeking superweapon in deep ruins; expedition may succeed (disaster if so)
- **Survival**: 10% for expeditions; requires shyak-powered wards (expensive)

#### **Spreading Zones** (Throughout Continent)
- **Growth**: 10% per year; accelerating
- **New Zones**: Appear randomly; no warning; villages consumed overnight
- **Evacuation**: Panic; refugees flood other regions; violence

---

### Natural Hazards

#### **Volcanic Eruptions** (Sulenor)
- **Frequency**: 1-2 major per year
- **Casualties**: 500-2,000 per eruption
- **Warning**: None; instant lava flows
- **Travel**: Routes through Sulenor require guides (500 GS); 30% never arrive

#### **Blizzards** (Vashor)
- **Frequency**: Daily in winter; weekly in summer
- **Casualties**: 100-500 per year (travelers frozen)
- **Survival**: Requires cold-weather gear (100 GS) + shyak heating (expensive)
- **Travel**: Winter routes closed 6 months/year

#### **Entropy Storms** (Konor, Expanding Elsewhere)
- **Frequency**: Daily in Konor; weekly in border regions
- **Effect**: Visible entropy waves; mutate anything caught; 100% fatality
- **Warning**: Sky darkens; creatures flee; 10 minutes to take cover
- **Survival**: Shyak wards (1,000 GS); underground shelters; prayer

#### **Swamp Diseases** (Eshtor)
- **Mortality**: 40% infection rate for travelers; 60% of those die
- **Symptoms**: Fever, hallucinations, flesh rot, death in 1-2 weeks
- **Prevention**: Herbal remedies (50 GS); arcane inoculation (500 GS; upper castes only)
- **Treatment**: Keshiel-Circle can cure (100 GS); if caught early

---

### Hostile Forces

#### **Vornalen-Keepers** (Eshtor Forests)
- **Territory**: Deep forests; 500,000 km²
- **Policy**: Kill outsiders; no exceptions; no warnings
- **Methods**: Ambush; arrows; creature attacks; vanish bodies
- **Casualties**: 200+ travelers per year
- **Bypass**: Stick to roads; hire Keeper-approved guides (rare; 5,000+ GS)

#### **Bandits & Pirates**
- **Locations**: All major trade routes; coasts
- **Frequency**: 1 attack per 100km traveled
- **Tactics**: Ambush caravans; demand ransom; kill if resist
- **Casualties**: 500-1,000 merchants per year
- **Defense**: Hire guards (100 GS per day per guard); travel in convoys

#### **Morshan-Breakers**
- **Targets**: Extractionist convoys; refinery shipments
- **Tactics**: Bombings; ambushes; no negotiations
- **Casualties**: 200+ extractionists per year
- **Defense**: Military escorts (expensive); avoid predictable routes

#### **Corrupted Creatures**
- **Locations**: Near entropy zones; expanding
- **Behavior**: Aggressive; attack on sight; often rabid/mad
- **Lethality**: 80% fatality if encounter without weapons
- **Frequency**: 1 encounter per 50km near dead zones
- **Defense**: Arcane firearms (expensive); avoid dead zones

## V. Regional Resources & Strategic Value

### Shyak Production (By Region)
1. **Sulenor**: 35% (fire-creatures)
2. **Eshtor**: 20% (forest/fungal creatures)
3. **Tavanor**: 20% (mixed habitats)
4. **Vashor**: 10% (ice-creatures; rare; high quality)
5. **Konor**: 5% (salvage; depleted sources)
6. **Dead Zones**: 10% (black market; salvaged from ancient reserves)

### Strategic Resources
- **Crystals** (Tavanor): Arcane conductors; essential for devices; Telvor-Shin controls
- **Metals** (Sulenor): Iron, copper, obsidian; weaponry; Koresh-Val controls
- **Lumber** (Eshtor): Construction, fuel; Velasha-Nen controls (conservation limits)
- **Food** (Eshtor/Tavanor): Fungal crops, fish, game; 60% of continent's food
- **Fresh Water** (Eshtor): Rivers, lakes; 70% of potable water; strategic weapon

### Control & Conflict
- **Koresh-Val wants Eshtor** (access to creatures + water)
- **Velasha-Nen defends Eshtor** (last healthy ecosystem)
- **Meshira-Tal desperate for anything** (Konor has nothing)
- **Threnok-Gash sells to highest bidder** (Vashor isolated; profits from conflicts)
- **Nethvor controls trade routes** (smuggling; extortion; information)

## VII. Hard R Regional Content

### Sulenor (Industrial Horror)
- **Refineries**: Workers die from fume exposure (lungs rot; blood boils); bodies dumped in lava
- **Mines**: Slaves worked to death (2-5 year lifespan); cave-ins bury hundreds; no rescue
- **Forge-Cities**: Children as young as 5 work furnaces; burned alive if fall in; daily occurrence

### Eshtor (Guerrilla Violence)
- **Keeper Ambushes**: Outsiders found with arrows in eyes, throats; hung from trees as warnings
- **Extractionist Retaliation**: Villages burned; civilians killed; children bayoneted; scorched earth
- **Swamp Deaths**: Travelers die slowly from disease; hallucinate; claw own eyes out; drown selves

### Vashor (Isolation Horror)
- **Freezing Deaths**: 100+ travelers freeze annually; found months later; perfectly preserved; terror on faces
- **Northern Vanishings**: 10,000 people gone; Nethvor's ritual (harvesting souls/flesh); evidence of torture before death
- **Cannibalism**: Some northern tribes resort to it; taboo broken; desperation

### Konor (Apocalyptic Despair)
- **Entropy Storms**: Victims mutate; flesh melts; bones twist; scream for hours; mercy-killed
- **Starvation**: 200+ die weekly; bodies piled; burned (if fuel available) or left
- **Cannibalism**: Confirmed; desperate eat dead; sometimes hunt living; authorities can't stop
- **Suicide**: Mass suicide pacts; entire families; 50+ per week; only escape

### Trade Routes (Predation)
- **Bandit Atrocities**: Merchants tortured for hidden valuables; families raped; children sold
- **Piracy**: Ships captured; crews thrown overboard; cargo stolen; no survivors
- **Slavery**: Travelers captured; sold to mines; families separated; never seen again

- **Vashor Glaciers** (North): Ice sheets 1km thick; expanding southward (ironic; entropy cold-resistant)

**Norbela is world of shrinking habitability. Travel is dangerous, expensive, and often fatal. Resources concentrate in baron hands. Dead zones expand. And every journey risks bandits, creatures, storms, or simply vanishing into entropy. Geography is weapon, and barons wield it brutally.**
