# Social Structures & Daily Life

**Genre**: Dark Fantasy / Arcane Punk / Hard R
**Focus**: Class stratification, exploitation, survival, vice, cultural norms, daily struggles

---

## I. Class System & Social Hierarchy

### The Seven Castes (Rigid Stratification)

#### 1. **Shyakor** (Arcane Elite) - ~1% of population
**Definition**: Barons, high-ranking officials, major guildmasters, wealthy merchants
**Privileges**:
- Unlimited magical oil access (personal reserves)
- Private armies/security forces
- Immunity from common law (baron's justice only)
- Palatial estates with arcane climate control, teleportation circles
- Access to forbidden knowledge/texts
- Right to harvest (own extraction territories)

**Daily Life**:
- Wake in silk sheets; servants dress them
- Breakfast: exotic foods from across continents (preserved via arcane cold storage)
- Attend council meetings, guild negotiations, plot against rivals
- Lunch: political dinners where fortunes change hands
- Afternoon: oversee operations via magical communication devices
- Evening: decadent parties, drug use (refined shyak derivatives), orchestras, theater
- Night: sleep in anti-entropy chambers (slow aging)

**Dark Elements**:
- Keep concubines (coerced through debt or threat)
- Casual cruelty to lower castes (beat servants for entertainment)
- Magical addiction common (pure shyak access leads to dependency)
- Incestuous politics (intermarry to consolidate power)
- Buy/sell political positions, including judgeships

**Hard R**:
- Sexual exploitation of servants, slaves, refugees
- Drug-fueled orgies; parties where "accidents" happen to inconvenient people
- Blood sports entertainment (prisoners vs. corrupted creatures)
- Casual murder covered up by bought officials

---

#### 2. **Meshkorin** (Skilled Professionals) - ~8% of population
**Definition**: Artificers, master craftsmen, scholars, physicians, military officers, established merchants
**Privileges**:
- Regular magical oil rations (enough for professional work, not luxury)
- Legal protections (can sue/be sued in baron's courts)
- Own property (homes, workshops, shops)
- Access to guilds/academies
- Can travel freely (with papers)

**Daily Life**:
- Wake in modest but comfortable homes (small arcane heating)
- Breakfast: bread, preserved meats, tea
- Work 10-12 hours in guildhalls, academies, workshops, clinics
- Lunch: guild cafeterias or street vendors
- Afternoon: continued work, guild meetings, politics within professional sphere
- Evening: family time (if can afford children), study, guild social events
- Night: sleep in warded beds (basic entropy protection)

**Struggles**:
- Constant pressure to produce; failure means demotion to lower castes
- Guild dues and licensing fees consume 30-40% of income
- Political games within guilds (backstabbing for promotions)
- Magical oil rationing means choosing between heating and professional work
- Fear of entropy corruption (exposure to magical materials)

**Dark Elements**:
- Many are addicted to low-grade shyak (performance enhancers)
- Burn out common; suicide rates high
- Sell children into apprenticeships (indentured servitude)
- Bribe officials constantly to maintain position

**Hard R**:
- Sexual favors traded for guild membership, promotions
- Workplace "accidents" arranged to eliminate competition
- Drug trade within guilds (stimulants, painkillers, shyak derivatives)

---

#### 3. **Telkorin** (Common Workers) - ~25% of population
**Definition**: Skilled laborers, small merchants, low-ranking soldiers, scribes, nurses, tradespeople
**Privileges**:
- Minimal magical oil rations (survival/work amounts only)
- Basic legal protections (rarely enforced)
- Can rent housing
- Access to public education (primary level only)
- Can petition for guild membership (rarely successful)

**Daily Life**:
- Wake in cramped apartments (4-6 people per room)
- Breakfast: gruel, stale bread, water
- Work 12-14 hours in shops, markets, barracks, manufactories
- Lunch: street food, whatever affordable
- Afternoon: continued work, no breaks
- Evening: exhausted collapse; maintain tools; family obligations
- Night: sleep on straw mattresses; cold; constant noise

**Struggles**:
- Never enough money; perpetual debt
- One illness/injury from poverty
- Children must work by age 8-10
- Landlords exploit them (arbitrary rent increases, evictions)
- No savings; live week-to-week

**Dark Elements**:
- Many turn to petty crime to survive
- Domestic violence common (stress, exhaustion, poverty)
- Alcoholism rampant (cheap moonshine everywhere)
- Sell possessions, sometimes family members, to pay debts
- Recruitment into gangs, insurgencies, cults

**Hard R**:
- Sex work supplements income for many
- Child labor normalized; children maimed in factories
- Loan sharks break legs, burn homes for missed payments
- Spousal/child abuse; no legal recourse

---

#### 4. **Korbal** (Unskilled Laborers) - ~35% of population
**Definition**: Factory workers, miners, dockworkers, servants, manual laborers, soldiers (rank-and-file)
**Privileges**:
- No magical oil rations (occasionally exposed through work)
- No legal protections (at mercy of employers)
- Cannot own property
- No education access
- Cannot travel without employer permission

**Daily Life**:
- Wake in barracks, tenements, or hovels (10-15 people per room)
- Breakfast: if lucky, gruel; often skip
- Work 14-16 hours in mines, refineries, docks, farms, factories
- Lunch: whatever employer provides (often spoiled/minimal)
- Afternoon: continued brutal labor
- Evening: too exhausted to do anything; collapse
- Night: sleep on floors, in shifts (bed-sharing), in cold

**Struggles**:
- Survival is only goal
- Hunger constant; malnutrition universal
- Injuries common; no medical care (unless employer pays)
- Death from overwork, accidents, exposure
- Life expectancy: 35-40 years

**Dark Elements**:
- Seen as disposable by employers
- Debt traps (company stores charge inflated prices; workers paid in scrip)
- Families broken up (sold to different employers)
- Despair universal; hope is luxury

**Hard R**:
- Industrial accidents horrific (crushed by machinery, burned alive, entropy mutations)
- Sexual assault by overseers common; no recourse
- Survival sex work (trade body for food, shelter)
- Children born into servitude; inherit parents' debts
- Cannabalism in worst famines (rumors, possibly true)

---

#### 5. **Drenkor** (Indentured Servants) - ~15% of population
**Definition**: Debtors, criminals, refugees, orphans, those who "volunteered" to escape starvation
**Status**: Legally property of creditor/employer for duration of contract (5-20 years, often extended)
**Privileges**: None; survival depends on owner's whim

**Daily Life**:
- Wake when ordered (often pre-dawn)
- Breakfast: scraps from owner's table, if anything
- Work: whatever owner demands (household labor, fieldwork, sexual services, factory work, mining)
- No breaks, no rest days, no time off
- Punishment for disobedience: beating, starvation, torture, sale to worse owner
- Sleep: wherever allowed (stables, basements, floors)

**Mechanisms of Indenture**:
- Debt: borrow money; can't repay; sign contract; trap extends indefinitely (interest, penalties, "living costs")
- Crime: caught stealing bread; choice is indenture or execution; choose indenture
- Refugee: flee dying regions; border guards offer "rescue" (indenture contract); sign or be turned away to die
- Orphan: no guardian; state assigns to employer; work until 25

**Dark Elements**:
- Suicide common (only escape)
- Owners trade/sell them like livestock
- Children born to indentured parents are also indentured (generational slavery)
- "Breeding programs" (owners force reproduction to create more servants)

**Hard R**:
- Sexual slavery widespread (servants used as concubines, then discarded)
- Brutal punishments (beatings, brandings, mutilations) for minor infractions
- Medical experiments on indentured by artificers (testing entropy effects, drug trials)
- Organs harvested from "troublesome" servants

---

#### 6. **Finyakor** (Outcasts) - ~10% of population
**Definition**: Exiles, mutants (entropy-corrupted), disgraced criminals, heretics, disease-carriers, untouchables
**Status**: No legal existence; can be killed without consequence
**Survival**: Scavenging, begging, banditry, living in ruins/forests/caves

**Daily Life**:
- Wake in ruins, sewers, forests, caves
- Breakfast: whatever can scavenge (rats, roots, garbage, sometimes corpses)
- Daytime: hide from authorities; scavenge; beg; steal; hunt
- Constant fear of violence (from everyone)
- Evening: find shelter; defend it from others
- Night: cold, hungry, terrified; sleep in shifts; guard against predators (beast and human)

**Dark Elements**:
- Many are entropy-mutated (extra limbs, glowing eyes, madness)
- Cannibalism confirmed (necessity, not choice)
- Form desperate gangs; prey on each other and lower castes
- Disease rampant; spreading to cities

**Hard R**:
- Violence is only law; murder/rape/torture common
- Children abandoned or killed (can't feed them)
- Trade in human flesh (both sexual slavery and literal consumption)
- Some willingly serve corrupted creatures (thralls) in exchange for scraps

---

#### 7. **Vordesh** (Slaves) - ~6% of population
**Definition**: Captured in war, born to slaves, sold by desperate families, criminals given life sentences
**Status**: Legal property; can be bought/sold/killed at will
**Primary Uses**: Mining, agriculture, domestic labor, sex work, gladiatorial combat, medical experiments

**Daily Life**:
- Wake to bell or whip
- No breakfast; fed once or twice per day (minimal)
- Work 16-18 hours under guard
- Beatings routine; both punishment and motivation
- No rest; no medical care unless valuable
- Sleep in pens, cages, barracks (chained if flight risk)

**Dark Elements**:
- Life expectancy: 2-5 years for mine slaves, 5-10 for agricultural, 1-3 for gladiators
- Families separated immediately on capture/sale
- Children born to slaves are slaves; often sold away from mothers at age 5
- Rebellions crushed with extreme prejudice (mass executions, crucifixions)

**Hard R**:
- Sex slaves (prostitution, forced breeding, personal use); discarded when no longer useful
- Gladiatorial games brutal (slaves vs. corrupted creatures, slaves vs. each other, execution spectacles)
- Medical experiments common (testing magical corruption, new drugs, entropy effects)
- Torture for entertainment (some nobles host private "shows")

## II. Cultural Norms & Social Rules

### Greetings & Courtesy
**Between Equals**: Use names + hand clasp; "Kesh telvor" (Joy to your works)
**To Superiors**: Bow; avert eyes; use title + "kelm-ov vesh" (I speak without fear/permission)
**To Inferiors**: Nod or ignore; no physical contact (contamination fear)

**Taboos**:
- Never touch someone of higher caste without permission (beaten or killed)
- Never look Baron directly in eyes (sign of challenge; execution)
- Never refuse direct order from superior caste (insubordination; punishment varies)

---

### Naming Conventions
**Upper Castes (Shyakor/Meshkorin)**: Full conlang names + lineage/title (Shalen-Mor tal Kalen-Sheloth)
**Middle Castes (Telkorin)**: Conlang first name + occupation (Grenash-Vel Kelkorin = Grenash-Vel the Speaker/Scribe)
**Lower Castes (Korbal)**: Single conlang name or nickname (Dren, Kesh, Vorn)
**Outcasts (Finyakor)**: Numbers, insults, or self-chosen names (usually descriptive: Scar, Twisted, Shadow)
**Slaves (Vordesh)**: Owners assign names or numbers; birth names erased

---

### Marriage & Family

#### Upper Castes
- **Arranged marriages** for political alliance
- **Dowries** involve territories, trade rights, magical oil reserves
- **Polygamy** common among Barons (wives + concubines)
- **Divorce** allowed but expensive; requires baron's court approval
- **Children**: Raised by servants/tutors; seen as political assets; groomed for power
- **Illegitimate children**: Acknowledged if useful; otherwise killed or exiled

#### Middle Castes
- **Arranged + romantic marriages** both occur
- **Dowries** modest (money, property, guild connections)
- **Monogamy** enforced socially (adultery = scandal but not illegal)
- **Divorce** possible but socially ruinous
- **Children**: 1-3 typical; expensive to raise and educate; often apprenticed away
- **Illegitimate children**: Hidden or integrated if marriage follows

#### Lower Castes
- **Informal partnerships** (legal marriage too expensive)
- **No dowries**; maybe token gifts
- **Monogamy** ideal but frequently broken (stress, desperation)
- **Divorce**: Informal; just leave
- **Children**: 4-8 born; 2-4 survive to adulthood; begin working age 6-8
- **Illegitimate children**: Common; no stigma; survival matters more than parentage

#### Indentured/Slaves
- **No legal marriages** (property can't marry)
- **Breeding programs** common (owners force partnerships)
- **Children**: Belong to owner; sold at age 5-10
- **Family bonds**: Desperately maintained but constantly broken

---

### Gender & Sexuality

#### Formal Stance
- **Patriarchal** upper castes (Barons almost always male; women inherit only if no sons)
- **Egalitarian-ish** middle/lower castes (survival more important than gender roles; women work same jobs as men)
- **Property status** for lower castes (both genders equally exploited)

#### Sexuality
- **Upper castes**: Bisexuality common; homosexuality tolerated if heirs are produced; sex is power/politics
- **Middle castes**: Heteronormativity enforced socially; LGBTQ+ people exist but hide
- **Lower castes**: No one cares; survival trumps orientation
- **Sex work**: Legal but stigmatized; controlled by guilds or criminals; exploitative

**Hard R**:
- **Sexual violence** widespread across all castes; rarely prosecuted
- **Consent** is privilege of upper castes; lower castes have no bodily autonomy
- **Children**: Sexual exploitation of minors common and unpunished among elites

---

### Food & Dining

#### Upper Castes (Shyakor)
- **Three+ elaborate meals** daily
- **Exotic foods**: Imported spices, tropical fruits (preserved via magic), creature meats (shera steaks), wines aged in arcane cellars
- **Dining rituals**: Multi-course; servants serve; courses paired with entertainment
- **Cost**: Single meal = Korbal's yearly wages

#### Middle Castes (Meshkorin)
- **Two meals** daily (breakfast and dinner)
- **Common foods**: Bread, cheese, preserved meats, root vegetables, beer, tea
- **Dining**: Family tables; modest but sufficient; occasional guild banquets
- **Cost**: 10-15% of income spent on food

#### Lower Castes (Telkorin/Korbal)
- **One meal + scraps** daily (if lucky)
- **Common foods**: Gruel, bread, whatever is cheapest (spoiled meat, moldy vegetables, rats)
- **Dining**: Eat standing or sitting on floors; no ceremony
- **Cost**: 50-60% of income spent on food; still not enough

#### Indentured/Slaves/Outcasts
- **Whatever scraps available**
- **Common foods**: Garbage, animal feed, bones for broth, insects, foraged plants
- **Dining**: Eat whenever allowed; competition for food
- **Cost**: N/A; begging, stealing, scavenging

**Cultural Foods**:
- **Shibak Stew**: Common worker food; fungal roots + preserved fish + grains; filling, bland
- **Vorn Bread**: Dark bread made from forest lichens; cheap, nutritious, bitter
- **Shyak-Infused Spirits**: Magically-charged alcohol; expensive; addictive; hallucinogenic

---

### Clothing & Fashion

#### Upper Castes
- **Materials**: Silk, velvet, furs from exotic creatures, arcane fabrics (self-cleaning, temperature-regulating)
- **Colors**: Rich dyes (crimson, royal blue, gold thread, silver embroidery)
- **Style**: Elaborate robes, cloaks, jewelry (magical), house/guild symbols
- **Cost**: Single outfit = Meshkorin's yearly wages

#### Middle Castes
- **Materials**: Wool, linen, leather
- **Colors**: Natural dyes (browns, greens, blues)
- **Style**: Practical; guild badges/symbols; modest jewelry
- **Cost**: 15-20% of yearly income for wardrobe

#### Lower Castes
- **Materials**: Rough wool, hemp, scavenged fabrics
- **Colors**: Undyed; grey, brown
- **Style**: Patched, worn, often second-hand; no ornamentation
- **Cost**: Whatever can afford; most clothing is recycled/found

#### Indentured/Slaves
- **Materials**: Sackcloth, rags
- **Colors**: Undyed; often branded with owner's mark
- **Style**: Uniform for slaves; whatever given to indentured; often naked in hot work environments
- **Cost**: Provided by owner (deducted from indenture debt)

---

### Housing

#### Upper Castes
- **Estates**: 50-200 rooms; marble, arcane architecture
- **Features**: Climate control, wards against entropy, teleportation circles, arcane lighting, hot/cold running water, private gardens, servant quarters
- **Security**: Private guards, magical alarms, moats/walls
- **Cost**: Multi-generational wealth; irreplaceable

#### Middle Castes
- **Townhouses/Apartments**: 3-8 rooms; brick/stone construction
- **Features**: Basic arcane heating (one room), manual water pump, oil lamps, shared courtyards
- **Security**: Guild guards, neighborhood watches
- **Cost**: Rent = 30-40% of income; ownership possible after 20 years

#### Lower Castes
- **Tenements**: 1-2 rooms; wood/clay construction
- **Features**: No heating; no running water (public wells); candles/fires; leaky roofs
- **Security**: None; frequent break-ins, violence
- **Cost**: Rent = 50-60% of income; evictions common

#### Indentured/Slaves
- **Barracks/Quarters**: Shared spaces (10-50 people); minimal structure
- **Features**: None; sleep on floors/straw; no privacy; no sanitation
- **Security**: Guards keep them in, not others out
- **Cost**: "Provided" by owner (debt increases)

#### Outcasts
- **Ruins/Caves/Forests/Sewers**: Whatever shelter can find/defend
- **Features**: None; exposure kills many
- **Security**: None; constant danger
- **Cost**: N/A; squatting

---

### Education

#### Upper Castes
- **Private tutors** from birth
- **Academies** (elite institutions; Telumesh Archive, Baron's Court Schools)
- **Curriculum**: History, politics, economics, arcane theory, warfare, diplomacy, arts
- **Outcomes**: Leadership positions guaranteed

#### Middle Castes
- **Guild apprenticeships** (ages 10-18)
- **Basic academics** (reading, writing, arithmetic) + trade skills
- **Curriculum**: Specialized by guild; rigorous
- **Outcomes**: Professional positions if successful; Korbal if fail

#### Lower Castes
- **Public schools** (ages 6-10; if accessible)
- **Basic literacy** + arithmetic (often poor quality)
- **Curriculum**: Minimal; focus on obedience
- **Outcomes**: Slightly better jobs than illiterate; still trapped in poverty

#### Indentured/Slaves/Outcasts
- **No formal education**
- **Skills learned on job** (if owner bothers to train)
- **Literacy rare**; seen as unnecessary or dangerous

---

### Healthcare

#### Upper Castes
- **Private physicians** (Keshiel-Circle elite members)
- **Arcane healing**: Magical surgery, life extension, anti-aging, regeneration
- **Cost**: Irrelevant; money no object
- **Life expectancy**: 80-100 years (with magic)

#### Middle Castes
- **Guild physicians** (Keshiel-Circle middle ranks)
- **Conventional medicine**: Surgery, herbs, basic healing magic (limited)
- **Cost**: 20-30% of yearly income per serious illness; bankrupting
- **Life expectancy**: 50-60 years

#### Lower Castes
- **Public clinics** (overcrowded, underfunded)
- **Herbal medicine**: Folk remedies, often ineffective
- **Cost**: Theoretically free; actually requires bribes; still unaffordable
- **Life expectancy**: 35-45 years

#### Indentured/Slaves/Outcasts
- **No healthcare** unless valuable
- **Die from infections, injuries, exposure**
- **Life expectancy**: 25-35 years (indentured), 20-30 years (slaves), 15-25 years (outcasts)

**Dark Elements**:
- Plague outbreaks common in lower castes; quarantine = lock them in to die
- Medical experiments on lower castes (sometimes without knowledge)
- Organ harvesting from executed criminals, dead slaves

## III. Daily Rhythms & Time

### Work Schedules
- **Upper Castes**: Flexible; "work" is social/political; 4-6 hours of "effort"
- **Middle Castes**: Dawn to dusk (10-12 hours); one rest day per week
- **Lower Castes**: Pre-dawn to post-dusk (14-16 hours); no rest days unless physically unable
- **Indentured/Slaves**: Whenever ordered; 16-18+ hours; no rest unless collapse

### Leisure Time
- **Upper Castes**: Theater, opera, blood sports, hunting (safaris for exotic corrupted creatures), drug parties, political salons
- **Middle Castes**: Guild gatherings, taverns, family time, reading, games (cards, dice)
- **Lower Castes**: Taverns (cheap ale), gambling, sex work, street performances, brawls
- **Indentured/Slaves/Outcasts**: No leisure; survival is full-time occupation

### Religious Observances
- **Festival Days**: 4 per year (seasonal); nominal rest days for middle/lower castes
- **Holy Sites**: Pilgrimage for those who can afford
- **Daily Prayers**: Upper castes have private chapels; lower castes pray at street shrines (if time)

## IV. Vice & Addiction

### Alcohol
- **Upper Castes**: Fine wines, arcane spirits; recreational use; rarely addiction (can afford treatment)
- **Middle Castes**: Beer, ale; social drinking; alcoholism emerging problem (stress)
- **Lower Castes**: Moonshine, rot-gut; epidemic alcoholism; only escape from misery
- **Cost**: Cheap alcohol is everywhere; expensive alcohol is status symbol

### Drugs
- **Shyak Derivatives**: Refined magical oil; extremely addictive; hallucinogenic; euphoric; enhances magic
  - **Pure Shyak**: Only upper castes; injected or inhaled; causes magical dependency + physical addiction
  - **Shirok** (low-grade): Workers use for stamina; causes jitters, insomnia, heart damage
  - **Shivesh** (corrupted): Outcasts use; causes mutations, madness, entropy corruption
- **Herbal Drugs**: Painkillers (opium-equivalent), stimulants, hallucinogens; legal but regulated
- **Addiction**: 15-20% of population dependent on some substance; no effective treatment for poor

**Hard R**:
- Addiction leads to theft, violence, prostitution
- Overdoses common; bodies found daily in lower-caste neighborhoods
- Children born addicted (mothers use during pregnancy); high mortality
- Drug trade controlled by Nethvor-Shadows + corrupt officials; profits immense

### Gambling
- **Upper Castes**: High-stakes games (fortunes won/lost in single night)
- **Middle Castes**: Tavern games (dice, cards); usually small stakes; some addicts lose everything
- **Lower Castes**: Street gambling (cards, dice, blood sports betting); predatory; debt traps
- **Cost**: Gambling debts lead to indenture; common pipeline into slavery

### Sex Work
- **Legal Status**: Regulated in cities; taxed; guild-controlled (exploitative guilds)
- **Upper-Tier**: Courtesans for upper castes; well-paid; some autonomy; still exploited
- **Mid-Tier**: Brothels for middle/lower castes; guild controls; workers indentured
- **Low-Tier**: Street work; unregulated; dangerous; survival prostitution
- **Child Exploitation**: Widespread; unpunished among elites; underground markets

## V. Crime & Punishment (Informal)

### Common Crimes by Caste

#### Lower Castes Commit:
- Theft (food, supplies, money)
- Fraud (false guild credentials)
- Assault (desperation, rage)
- Illegal gathering (unions forbidden)
- Trespassing (squatting in ruins)

**Punishment**:
- Beating
- Mutilation (hand cut off for theft)
- Indenture (debt servitude)
- Execution (hanging, burning for serious crimes)

#### Middle Castes Commit:
- Fraud (business, magical goods)
- Bribery (officials, guilds)
- Assault (duels, feuds)
- Smuggling (black market goods)

**Punishment**:
- Fines (heavy)
- Guild expulsion
- Indenture (rare)
- Execution (only for treason, murder)

#### Upper Castes Commit:
- Everything (murder, torture, rape, slavery, treason)

**Punishment**:
- Rarely prosecuted; money buys immunity
- House arrest (if caught by other Barons)
- Rarely: execution (only if Baron demands, for political reasons)

## VI. Childhood & Coming of Age

### Upper Castes
- **Birth**: Celebrated; heirs are political assets
- **Childhood**: Sheltered; educated by tutors; no manual labor
- **Coming of Age**: 16; formal ceremonies; given positions in family enterprises
- **Expectations**: Marry well; consolidate power; continue family legacy

### Middle Castes
- **Birth**: Celebrated if family can afford another child
- **Childhood**: Basic education; begin learning trade at 8-10
- **Coming of Age**: 14; begin apprenticeship or military service
- **Expectations**: Master trade; marry within class; support family

### Lower Castes
- **Birth**: Often unwanted; burden
- **Childhood**: Work begins at 6; no formal education; frequent hunger
- **Coming of Age**: 10-12; childhood ends; full labor expected
- **Expectations**: Survive; contribute to family; unlikely to advance

### Indentured/Slaves
- **Birth**: Property; separated from mother at age 5-10
- **Childhood**: Labor from age 4-5; brutal conditions
- **Coming of Age**: None; life is labor until death
- **Expectations**: Obey; work; die without causing trouble

---

## VII. Death & Mourning

### Upper Castes
- **Death Rites**: Elaborate funerals; body preserved via magic; entombed in family crypts
- **Mourning**: 1 year of formal mourning; heirs wear black; political maneuvering immediate
- **Belief**: Death is transition; magic can extend life; some seek immortality

### Middle Castes
- **Death Rites**: Traditional funerals; body cremated or buried; guild ceremonies
- **Mourning**: 3 months; family wears mourning colors; life continues
- **Belief**: Death is natural; honored dead remembered; some afterlife belief

### Lower Castes
- **Death Rites**: Quick burial (mass graves if plague); informal memorials
- **Mourning**: 1 week if lucky; can't afford more time off work
- **Belief**: Death is release from suffering; hope for better next life

### Indentured/Slaves/Outcasts
- **Death Rites**: None; bodies dumped in mass graves or left for scavengers
- **Mourning**: Informal; quiet; grief is dangerous (distracts from work)
- **Belief**: Death is only freedom; many welcome it

---

## VIII. Dark Fantasy & Arcane Punk Elements in Daily Life

### Arcane Punk Aesthetics
- **Magical Technology**: Everywhere but unevenly distributed; upper castes have convenience; lower castes have exploitation
- **Refineries**: Dominate cityscapes; belch corrupted smoke; workers die young
- **Streetlights**: Arcane lamps (powered by shyak); flicker; sometimes fail; bodies found beneath them
- **Communication**: Magical messaging stones (upper castes); messengers (middle/lower castes)
- **Transport**: Arcane carriages (upper castes); magical rail lines (middle castes; expensive); walking (lower castes)

### Entropy in Daily Life
- **Visible**: Sections of cities are "dead zones" (no magic; unsafe)
- **Corruption**: Random mutations; people born wrong; killed or exiled
- **Fear**: Constant; entropy could consume region any time; no warning
- **Adaptation**: Some worship entropy; some fight it; most just endure

### Body Horror
- **Industrial Accidents**: Magical corruption; workers transform; usually fatal
- **Mutants**: Finyakor caste full of entropy-warped; tentacles, extra eyes, madness
- **Medical**: Healers sometimes fail; magical healing goes wrong; flesh fuses incorrectly; agony

### Moral Ambiguity
- **No Heroes**: System is broken; everyone complicit; survival requires compromise
- **Sympathetic Villains**: Baron Koresh-Val is dying; wants legacy before death; willing to destroy world for it
- **Villainous Heroes**: Morshan fights oppression; kills innocents; both true
- **Oppression**: Lower castes oppressed but also oppress those below them (Korbal look down on Finyakor)

---

**Daily life in Norbela is brutal, stratified, and steeped in exploitation. Magic makes elites comfortable and everyone else disposable. Hope is rare. Violence is common. And everyone is one disaster away from falling into the abyss.**
